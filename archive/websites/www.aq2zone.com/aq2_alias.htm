What is aliasing?

 
Basically, aliasing is combining a series of commands to make one single command. Let's look at an example:
alias reportin "say_team I'm still standing!; radio reportin; wave 3"
bind j reportin
This makes it so whenever you press j, you will say to your team I'm still standing!, you'll play the Team, report in! radio sound and players will see you wave. The big thing you need to learn is that to separate commands you put ; in.
At it's most basic, all you need to know to alias is:
alias thing "command1; command2; command3"
The crucial parts which you cannot change are highlighted in orange. You must have all the commands you want contained within the speech marks, or it won't work. Remember, you need to have ; separating commands. You do not have too have three commands, it can be less or more but if the line becomes too long it will not work, so I stick to three or four and try to keep them short
More advanced aliasing

 
Take a look at the alias I use for when a teammate is killed in:
alias teamdown1 "say_team Don't look at me, I didn't kill him/her (delete as applicable); radio teamdown; bind rightarrow teamdown2"
alias teamdown2 "say_team I didn't kill him/her (delete as applicable), honestly!; radio teamdown; bind rightarrow teamdown3"
alias teamdown3 "say_team It's worse than that he's dead, Jim!; radio teamdown; bind rightarrow teamdown1"
bind rightarrow teamdown1
It may look complex in a big bulk, but look at it one line at a time, it's no more complex then the report in alias we looked at above. The first time you press the rightarrow key, it plays the radio message Teammate down!, says to your team Don't look at me, I didn't kill him/her (delete as applicable), then binds the rightarrow key to the teamdown2 command. Remember that ; separates commands.

You'll notice that teamdown2 is a similar alias, but at the end it binds the rightarrow key to teamdown3. At the end of teamdown3, it binds rightarrow to teamdown1. This means that when all three messages have been used, it goes back to the top one and the loop starts again
What to do when something goes bad

 
When you start using a new alias, you may find things won't work right, like you won't do all the commands, or it'll say the word which was meant to be a command. Most of the time this is down to spelling mistakes kill18 can easily be mis-spelled as kil18, and you won't even notice until you say kil18 in a game instead of carrying out the line of the alias

So first of all, go to the line which is causing problems and check it carefully for spelling mistakes. If there are no spelling mistakes in that line, check the line before it too. If that fails, maybe the line is too long, Quake 2 only supports lines up to a certain length, the maximum is around 135 characters. If your line is longer than that, cut it down to size. If you still can't figure it out, mail the whole alias to me and I'll do my best to fix it

One thing I do when testing a new alias is to load up a map in AQ2, then simply go through the whole alias. If I notice an error I quit AQ2, fix it, then check the rest until I'm sure it is all working

If you see the error message "Line has unmatched quote, discarded." when you load AQ2, it means that you have an imcomplete alias, you forgot to close the speech marks at the end of the alias. Open up your config and look for any alias without the speech marks at the end, then add them in
