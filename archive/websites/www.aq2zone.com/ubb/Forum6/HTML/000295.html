I had a quick look using r_drawflat 1.

In the front of the building, part of the problem is the brush splitting of the ground that you're getting around the pillars and the irregular shaped bits around the doors. Try reducing the the number of faces on the pillars (even a 6-sides cylinder looks ok if you use the -smooth option in RAD).

Around the doors and windows, try making the details out of a single flat texture on a few brushes rather than different textures on lots of brushes. It won't look as good but it'll be a close approximation.

I'm fairly sure func_walls are epolys.

A "splitting brush" is one which splits up the brushes it is touching (or sometimes even brushes it is near). Use r_drawflat 1 on pretty much any map and you should be able to see the effect no problem.

I personally use func_walls for anything that will create more polys through splitting than would be added by making the thing a func_wall. Not things that should make an appreciable shadow though. Basically this means things like table legs, clocks, etc.

clarification time:

W_Poly, also known as World Poly
Consists of EVERYTHING in the map that is based on brushes withing the .bsp file.

E_Poly, also known as Entity Poly
Consists of EVERYTHING spawned from the map and engine that is based on .mdl files.

A brush based entity is still a world poly, for the due fact that the engine renders it from the BSP.

Made Man 		posted 02-27-2000 07:34 PM            
r_speeds suck, and HL is very sensitive about them. I suggest you leave a 1 unit of space between every two complicated world brushes, change everything that is not covering visibility much into entities, stay away from small brushes, make your brushes fairly big. small ones may split other brushes into small brushes, and rspeeds go nuts. stretch textures, or better yet, get some better textures from sites, or take a texture you have, export it in wally to like Paint, and make it 256x256. then it wont split things up so much. compil;e you map as HL single player, start up in developers, and type r_draworder 1 in the console. (and make sure you're running in doftware video mode, only works in that). You should see how much of your map is being drawn from where. often the enigne draws around corners and stuff. Block that with brushes. And dont forget the engine draws thru all entities, like doors, so having a door between 2 highly complex areas wont stop the r_speeds. Lastly, use hint brushes in some places. GO to the HL Editing Resource center http://halflife.gamedesign.net/ and look aorund if you havent yet, they have some good stuff. Hope this helps.
