Made Man 		posted 02-28-2000 06:10 PM            
It's called breach of copyright; you may have heard of it.

It's actually a felony in the US (i.e. a criminal offence you can be arrested for) if you distribute many copies (>10) of a work and charge people for it over a certain sum ($2000 I think).

Even if you don't charge anyone for it, it's a civil offence which could get you sued. And the damages could be quite heavy if you harm the commerical value of the work. Giving the company you are stealing from credit won't cut any ice.

Whether any company would actually bother suing you is a different matter, but if they found out they might at least send you threatening legal letters. They have lawyers on a retainer fee anyway, they might as well get some use out of them.

You might not think it would happen, but try asking any of the mod authors who got 'foxed'. I believe the Opera guy (Nighthawk?) used to work on a Star Wars mod that got shut down. Trademark rather than copyright violation in that case, but the principle is the same.

Finally, it is lame to try justifying stealing intellectual property by saying that you aren't good enough to create it yourself. Learn how. Yes, it's hard (God^H^H^HGoat knows I am no great shakes as a texture artist) but it's no more difficult than any other aspect of mapping. And isn't it more satisfying to create something yourself than to rip off someone else? Otherwise you might as well re-name someone else's map and distribute it as your own.

Also, look around the net. There are a number of useful, freely-distributable texture sites around (some are even linked from the Map Depot.) Although they may not be exactly to your taste, you can use them as a base to make modified versions. (I find it much, much easier to modify a texture than to create one from scratch.) Or, try asking if someone would like to collaborate with you and do some texture creation.

Sorry if this comes over as a flame; it was meant to be constructive. But I'm really fed up of the old "If I don't charge for it everything's fine" canard.

-- Dr Bob
