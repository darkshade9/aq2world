A while ago there was a discussion here about whether the HL engine had an equivalent of Q2's very useful gl_showtris. This is useful since it lets you see just what parts of your map the engine is drawing, and lets you optimise hint brush placement etc.

The nearest we got in HL was r_draworder 1, which draws the brushes 'backwards', nearest first. It works, but it's pretty hard to see exactly what is going on, plus it only works in the software rendering mode. There is also r_drawflat, which shows you the way your brushes are split - also only available in software.

Well, I've discovered another one, after reading about the 'wall cheat' in the CS forums. The wall cheat makes walls somewhat transparent, and makes your PVS obvious. This is really useful to mappers, but of course not so great for the game overall. It's actually better than the old gl_showtris, since it's less confusing. And it works without switching to software mode (though only in D3D, not OpenGL, on my TNT2).

I put up a few screenshots of it turned on:


(Incidently, the second one shows that rats could really do with some better brush placement to stop the inside of the cupboard showing...)

I'm kind of reluctant to spell out what the command for the cheat is, since its so open to abuse (unlike r_draworder, the cheat can be used in an online game.) Fortunately I think it's less of an issue in AHL than in CS, but it's still an unfair advantage to those using it.

However,if you go over to the CS forums you'll probably find at least 500 posts with exactly what the command is. Use it while you can, mappers, since valve are supposedly dissabling it with the next patch (I hope they'll keep it in for non-online games so mappers can still use it, but unfortunately I doubt it.)

-- Dr Bob

Oh, belive it or not, the 'wall cheat' is still useful in HL 1.0.1.8 (the new patch).
