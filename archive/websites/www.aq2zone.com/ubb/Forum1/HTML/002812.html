I make all my own textures now.
It definitely allows you more control over how well you can get a scene from imagination to the editor, because you can make textures that suit the scene instead of having to compromise and put in a texture that is only kinda like what appears in your head.

My little bit of advice from experience:

I use paintshop pro, and make the texture at large scale (1024x1024) and 24 bit color. Then you copy it, paste as a new texture, use that copy and resize and drop the color depth to 256 (much better done in a paint program than automatically by the .wal builders).

Then I use Wally to convert it to the proper format... that includes creating the mipmaps.

This technique is useful in all engines, and results in the best quality output.
