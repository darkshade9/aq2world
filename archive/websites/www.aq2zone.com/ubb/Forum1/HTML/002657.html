I hung out a bit on some Aussie servers recently just to see what it's like.

The players seem to be pretty friendly, though very clique-ish. I had a hard time getting any responses to my questions at first. After sticking around a while gradually more people talked to me.

At one point someone called me a lame camper because I killed someone while I was busy weapon farming in an enclosed room. Hey, someone's gotta test the AQ2:Labyrinth version (1.52+Vote). I actually killed him with a 600 ping to his 50. Hahah! I then asked him how well he'd play with a 600 ping.

After that, things quieted down.

They tend to spam a bit, though some of the more experienced or better players HATE the players who spam.

They aren't as experienced as the US players, generally. They don't move as well. They are pretty handy accuracy-wise, but seem to be on a slightly lower level overall than the average US player.

I'm not saying they suck, there are some pretty good players. It's just a different, more sedate and slower-paced playing style.

Clans I encountered: [tDM], [DBz], [smoke], and some other ones I forget the names of. The players were pretty good and friendly, once I got them to acknowledge me.

Yes, in Australia matches are called "Wars". That's their terminology, nothing wrong with it, eh.

Just thought some people would be interested in my experiences with 'em, what with this Aussie bloke here. The players I encountered were friendlier and less of punks than this guy, so don't judge their community by him. Also, one of the servers I played on didn't record frags, which I found interesting. And they really REALLY like the map ACTDAM.
