Made Man 		posted 10-19-2000 11:28 AM            
Soundman, the I2 will have a fiber optic backbone just as before, *but* it will be a faster FO line. That doesn't mean that suddenly, you will be able to have a fiber optic connection in your home. That doesn't mean that everyone will upgrade their servers. They won't, and you won't. There will be some across-the-board speed improvements, especially in cross-continental latency, but overall, it's not going to let you squeeze every drop of bandwidth outta your DSL. Factors that will still limit your bandwidth:
Your connection
Your ISP's connection to the backbone
The server's ISP's connection to the backbone
The server's connection
Remember, most connections to the cross-continental backbone are not direct; do a trace route on a server in the next provicne/state, and you will see the many hops your data takes.</BS>
