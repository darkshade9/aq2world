Intel Press Release

Intel made another press release about their Pentium III launch date. Here it is.

U.S. microprocessor group Intel Corp. will officially launch its new Pentium III product on Feb. 26 following a big Feb. 17 preview event in San Jose, Calif. An Intel spokeswoman said Thursday that after the launch date computer manufacturers would have PCs with Pentium III available for sale. She added that Intel had not yet decided on the pricing of the Pentium III but that it should be the mainstream product of the company by the end of 1999. The Pentium III will be launched with clock rates of 450 to 500 megahertz. Intel aims to boost the clock rates of its products to one gigahertz, but the spokeswoman said it was not sure whether this would be a Pentium III version or another product

Pentium 500 review

There's a review of the PIII 500 Mhz over at PlanetHardware here's a little from the article.

The other main rumors circling around the Pentium III were than it would ship with 64k of Level One cache (compared to 32k for Pentium MMX and Pentium II, 16k for original Pentium's), and that the out the door front side bus speed would be set at 133MHz, both of which Intel failed to deliver on. The Pentium III ships with 32k of L1 cache, and will also run at the standard 100MHz front side bus speed, both of which are no improvement over the Pentium II. Compare this to the AMD K7 chip, which will run at a blistering 200MHz bus speed, and 128k of Level 1 cache, and you've got a losing battle on Intel's part. We'll have to wait and see if the second revisions of the Pentium III can compete with the K7, but it's for sure if they leave the PIII as is, the K7 will smoke it off the road.

Benchmarks from 3DSMax

3DSMax has some benchmarks for a Win NT, 98, running dual processors. Here's what you can expect. A bit from the conclusion.

According to the benchmarks run on my machine, the addition of a second processor nets an average of an 89% speed increase in rendering, and a 35-40% increase in viewport modeling. Overall this means that your almost going to half any and all renders that your performing. And considering a 67,000 polygon 100 frame animation can take almost 303 minutes with one PII 504, which means that's a huge save in time. Additional benefits are also seen when one starts multitasking between multiple applications. I myself had never used a dual processor system until recently, the performance gain in Windows NT is nothing compared to the difference in feel.

3Dfx Press Release

3Dfx announced their graphics boards with Voodoo3 2000, 3000, and 3500. Here's a bit from the press release.

"The Voodoo3 family of products will be our first 3Dfx-branded boards and we are committed to delivering the best possible experience for our customers," said Greg Ballard, president and CEO for 3Dfx Interactive. Industry-Leading Performance

Manufactured by STB Systems, Inc., Voodoo3 3000 designed for true 3D game enthusiasts, will feature a 350MHz RAMDAC, TV/S-Video Out capabilities as well as a yet undisclosed game bundle featuring best-selling titles. The Voodoo3 3000 will also feature 16 MB of SDRAM. The Voodoo3 is expected to lead in real-world game performance by delivering super-fast frame rates for the most popular games.

Clock speeds and PS drivers

Over at Entech this little bit was spotted about the Powerstrip utility.

For the record, and contrary to what you might read elsewhere: (1) drivers can adjust clock speed and override BIOS defaults, but STB drivers in general and the latest TNT Detonator drivers in particular do not do this; (2) the G200 does boot up at 125MHz, dropping to 112.5MHz only at resolutions and color depths that could be used for 3D (a perfectly reasonable thing to do, in the interests of stability); (3) although there are numerous registry-based clock controls around now, the PowerStrip is not one of them and the latest release will delete any clock values it finds in the registry.

DX6 & Supercharged

Chris at VE is testing one of the new Savage powered boards by Hercules, Supercharged, and came across some small problems along with the how to fix it.

The Savage3D has computability problems with some DX6 games that are remedied when you install DX6.1. However, it gains certain image quality problems in the process. I noticed them in SimCity 3000, but they affect other programs as well - usually DDraw-related, but many D3D programs use DDraw for some things, so it's still relevant (you can run SC3000 on a Voodoo2! Its not 3D, but wtf...). Apparently it's a problem with a leftover registry key that was needed in DX6 but not in 6.1. If you've encountered the screen flashing/tearing problems in DX6 apps and you have a Savage3D, you should install DX6.1 and then:

Run regedit and open HKEY_CURRENT_CONFIG\Display\Settings. You should see a registry entry "TP" which is set to "ON". REMOVE this registry entry entirely and restart the machine and you're all set
