Your first question is probably along the lines of "What the hell is this about?" Well, each month we will interview the creator of a map that is recognized as the epitome of AQ maps. Your next question is probably "How do you determine what is a top map?" Well, we informally polled several experienced AQ2 fraggers, server ops, and asked them what they felt was the top DM and TP maps that have been around since AQ2 was born and became the kick-ass mod that it is.

Last month we found out how Kumanru was built. This month we welcome Ruskprick, mapper of such hits as 'Towel for Tuba' (which is actually a Q2 map but should be converted to AQ2 since it looks so cool); 'Teacher', which has some really cool textures; and his latest submission 'City'. What really sticks out about Rukprick's maps is the high quality of all his textures, the intricate designs, and all the great prefabs that he puts in his maps. Just after our move to Telefragged, we spoke with Ruskprick about how and why he builds AQ2 maps, what's the key to making cool maps, and we also get some hints about his latest AQ2 projects.

[AQMD] Welcome to "the SPOTLIGHT"! and please welcome Ruskprick, mapper of 'The City'. Nice to have ya by AQMD for a "visit".

[Ruskprick] Thanks....

[AQMD] Where'd you usually get the ideas to make your maps?

[Ruskprick] Usually when I watch a movie or play a new game I get new ideas. Sometimes when I visit some place I get inspired, I see the whole world as a big Quake-map. (Maybe I should visit a shrink?) Before Quake2 I played a lot of Duke Nukem and I like big open spaces where you hardly can see the other players when they are at the other side of the map. Most of my ideas aren't possible to make with the Quake2-engine.

[AQMD] What was your first map?

[Ruskprick] The first one was "Warlock's lair", when I made that map I wanted to make as much cool effects and different color-combinations as possible. The layout for the map was very weird with a lot of secret passages between the rooms, it's a miracle that the map works at all when I used too much of the function "csg substract" which made a lot of leaks in the map. I spent alot of time fixing it. Don't use that function too much!

[AQMD] How long had you been making maps before you made City?

[Ruskprick] It's been about 1 year since I started making "Warlock's lair". After that one I made a map called "Madhouse", and that's exactly what it is. The r_speeds are way too high, download the map as an example on how to NOT make maps. Then I made "A towel for Tuba" after an idea from my friend Tuba. I thought that it was too many Quake-style maps out and I wanted to do something original. I've recieved some e-mails telling me to send it to Mapdepot but I don't think the map would work very well in Teamplay. It should take too much time to find each other in it, and no one seems to play Deathmatch anymore. The next map I made was "The search for wulfhere". I haven't seen anyone running the map on any servers, but I think the map should work fine for the mod "Capture the chicken". After making those maps I discovered Action Quake, i guess I would have erased my Quake2-directory if I hadn't found this great mod. Then I made the map "When I killed the teacher". It took two months to make it because I decided to use only my own textures. When I made the layout I wanted it to work for "Capture the flag" and I had some different versions of the map. When I uploaded the map I messed things up and sent a bad version, that's why the sounds for it don't work and there's a spawn-place that's f****d up. It's primary made for deathmatch and I think it's too many rooms for teamplay.

[AQMD] What was the most difficult part of the map to make? Why?

[Ruskprick] When I made the city-map I wanted to make a map made only for teamplay with an easy layout. The hard part was to make realistic textures. I found some books about houses at the library wich I used for inspiration. Some inspiration comes from the movie "Karlsson pa taket" (Karlsson on the roof) and that's why I put the tune from the film in one of the rooms. I didn't like the tune from the beginning but now I play it on my stereo everyday when I'm making my new maps. Maybe there's some subliminal messages in it that makes me play it over and over again?

[AQMD] What was the easiest part of the map to make?

[Ruskprick] The easiest part was the layout.

[AQMD] Do you have a general basis in mind when you design your maps? Was there an example you wanted to emulate?

[Ruskprick] There's no special place that I've tried to make. I try to make maps that people will recognize from where they live or from some movies. The City map was made to look like a typical Swedish town or suburb.

[AQMD] What are your favorite and least favorite areas of your newest map - City?

[Ruskprick] I like the room with the Karlsson-tune. I like it because there you can listen to the tune while you shoot people on the street. The first time I played the map on the Internet I wondered where everybody was and I found both teams in the Karlsson-room dancing to the tune, that's what I call teamplay. :-) My least favorite place is the police-station since I have not had a very good relation to the local police. (That's why I'm never gonna make a prison-map, it would give too many bad memories)

[AQMD] As you map, what's the order you think of things as being most important: playability, flow, atmosphere?

[Ruskprick] I think everything is very important, but the most important thing is that the map is fun to play.

[AQMD] What software and hardware did you use to make City?

[Ruskprick] I use Qoole. I've tried other editors but I think Qoole is the one that's easiest to use. At the moment I have a P300, 96mb RAM, dual Voodoo2 and a lot of other stuff.

[AQMD] How long did it take to compile City?

About 1 hour. (Swedish time)

[AQMD] How much time do you invest into a map, from the first brush to the last compile?

[Ruskprick] I guess I invest too much time in making details that no one will ever notice when playing, but I want everything to look great. Totally I make about 50-60 compiles from the start to the finish. That's to check the layout and lighting. It's good to save the map with different filenames so that you don't have to start all over if things get wrong. The last thing I do on a map is to put the sound and place the entities. I usually start to make a plot of the map with pen and paper before I get started with the editor. I'm lucky to have two computers so I can play Action Quake while I compile the maps.

[AQMD] Who's your favorite texture artist or do you do your own?

[Ruskprick] I don't know about any texture-artists. I make all my textures by myself.

[AQMD] If you use a texture artists work - why do you use theirs?

[Ruskprick] I only use my own textures and the default quake2-textures.

[AQMD] If you do your own textures - what software do you usually use?

[Ruskprick] For the textures I use Wally and PSP.

[AQMD] Are you presently working on any other maps for AQ2?

[Ruskprick] I sure am. I'll finish it when I'm finished with a new version of the city-map that I'm modifying for CTB right now. Foolkiller asked me to make a new version of it and he gave me some hints on how to change it for CTB. The other new map that I'm working on is a CTB/Teamplay-map dedicated to Saddam Hussein (A fellow from the middle-east). It will feature alot of traps, a SCUD-missile, chemical weapons and other stuff. I hope I'm not going to get a prize on my head if Saddam and his friends don't like the map. (I've got inside information that Saddam is a dedicated Quake2-player)

[AQMD] Are there any unfinished projects?

[Ruskprick] I usually finish my projects.

[AQMD] What else do you do when not making maps?

[Ruskprick] I work at Volvo in Skovde assembling car-engines to earn money that I spend at technical toys and booze. I also listen to hardrock and metal-music. (And Karlsson)

[AQMD] Any recommendations for the new mappers out there?

[Ruskprick] 1. Check out Rust's homepage. There's all the tutorials you need to get started.

2. Try to keep r_speeds low. (Check out the tutorial "polygon count reduction methods" at Rust's)

3. Take 5 minute breaks every hour. If you don't you could get pain in your eyes.

[AQMD] Thanks for stopping in with us and good luck on your new projects - if they look as sweet as your others, they're gonna rock!

[Ruskprick] Have a nice life!
