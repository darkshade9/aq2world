Reviewed By: PlastiKman

Review

Architecture Rating: 6/10
Architecture Comments:
Hey boys and girls! Rookie here again telling it how it is!

Well, to say I'm not a Ruskprick fan is an outright lie. He is probably one of the best mappers out there but there is no denying that but Saddam, well, isn't quite up to "par."

The layout for Saddam is decent enough but glaring errors exsists. One is the openess of the map. There is WAY too much open space. If there was more cover it'd be great. Also the map really caters to CTB rather then DM or even TP, the map is just too BIG for any other kind of game. However this is an AMAZING CTB map. With all the keen traps and multiple routes from each side of the stage Saddam is an enjoyable CTB level.

Looks Rating: 10/10
Looks Comments:
The return of Ruskpricks gourgeous high colour textures is back into full effect. The map looks absolutely stunning! The attention to detail is quite amazing from the Iraq flags on the crates to the pipes in the walls. Just by looking at it you know it's a Ruskprick level.

Gameplay Rating: 5/10
Gameplay Comments:
This is where Saddam dies. On a gameplay level Saddam in nowhere near as fun as City, Teacher or CTBcity. It seems Ruskprick put too much attention into the "traps" rather then making a design that would accomadate flawless gameplay. The result? You spend the fist few minutes goofing around with the traps till you realzie, no one falls for them.

Ambiance Rating: 10/10
Ambiance Comments:
Once again, this look, sounds, feel of this level just seems like I'm running through an Iraqi missle silo, alebeit an possible parody of an Iraqi missle silo. The industrial conveyor belts, the crates, the cold metallic look of the halls add to the ambiance which can only be described as "stunning."

Other Comments:

Conclusion:
While beautiful Saddam is a beautiful map is falls into the "all flash no substance" area. Once you have foon ooing and aweing you realize this is a pretty static map. "Raise the bar" you say? Maybe not.

Overall Rating: 7
