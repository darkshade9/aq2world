Review

Architecture Rating: 10/10
Architecture Comments:
This is another awesome map in the Urban series. Even though the three of them all have huge similiarities, each has characteristics that make them unique. In Urban3, many people would look at it for the first time and think "this map is catered to campers." Yeah, that's only if you're a rusher with his thumb up his ass. I was playing this map against a team of two SGG toters, and I wasted them plenty with my MP5. You just have to keep moving, and the prensense of ladders and jumpable allies between buildings makes that possible.

Looks Rating: 7/10
Looks Comments:
I really liked that for once in a level it's based in day light, not some dreary nightime or twilight haze. It gave the level a refreshing feeling. It really does have the feeling of a city. The only reason I gave it a 7 is that I would like to see some more city addtions, like parked cars and a few stores you can duck into. Right now it's a very functional level, but if you have the oppurtunity to look around during the game... you'll notice it's very bear.

Gameplay Rating: 10/10
Gameplay Comments:
Campers and Rushers (well, myself) seem to like this level. It isn't cramped (I think it may be the biggest map I've played) and it has many rooftop cover points (vents, lots of vertical change). Also, unlike ActCity, there isn't any point where the escape from a high point isn't obvious, which is good in team play so you don't plummet to your death. Lots of fun to play.

Ambiance Rating: 3/10
Ambiance Comments:
This is deffinetly where this level lacks. It has no ambiance. Not a single sound or anything. Like I said, it's a super functional map... but other than that, it's rather uninteresting to fumble around by yourself (but who ever does? these maps are at their peak when you realize a good way to waste people)

Other Comments:
Even though some of my ratings were low, this is a great map. A pioneer for other HUGE maps.

Conclusion:
Ooops, kind of said it up there. Good map... pioneer.

Reviewed By: [NB]cornholio

Review

Architecture Rating: 7/10
Architecture Comments:
Urban is back! This time in full daylight. The buildings remind me of the previous maps in the Urban series, which is a nice thing since their desing is simplistic, but works very well nonetheless. The layout is basically three main rooftops (with spawn points), the connecting roofs, and the street beneath them. Only one thing hinders the layout, robbing it of a nice 8 or 9. This is the fact that the rooftops are only reachable by two sets of ladders, and if a skilled and controlled enough team spawns on top of the highest spawn point, it can easily control the other team's upward access and pick them off one by one when they try to come up.

Looks Rating: 5/10
Looks Comments:
The textures are rather fine and do not irritate the eye, but they are getting a bit old, aren't they? I mean, Gerbil! has recycled these same textures in every Urban series map and, frankly, I've had enough of them. _If_ Urban4 is being planned, please switch the textures. Also, the sky is way too bright.

Gameplay Rating: 8/10
Gameplay Comments:
Joy! I've found use for every weapon in the list on this map. Spray-toys are self-explanatory, and everybody knows snipers are abundant on the rooftops of all urbans. What surprises me the most is that even though this is a daylight map, stealth slippers and a M90 or a Hand Cannon can cause a lot of damage. It seems that rooftop snipers tend to think that nobody can come behind them. They do, however, learn after a few rounds of HC-aided flights... All in all, excluding the occasional camper, this map is a delight.

Ambiance Rating: 5/10
Ambiance Comments:
No sounds, no catchy details, no nothing. To me it seems that Urbans have always relied on the Fun Factor to patch up what they lack in the extras department, and it works. While playing the Urban series maps I have never missed any sounds or lighting effects; the fun replaces them well enough.

Other Comments:
Change the sky, give us a third ladder leading up and remove the spawn point from the "box" on the plateau and TA-DA(!), a perfect map. Oh, and a good ping helps a lot in this map...

Conclusion:
Good work Gerbil!! A fine addition to the Urban series. (If you hate the previous Urbans, you will hate this too, I'm sure)

Reviewed By: Scinet .L.

REVIEW
Architecture & Layout score 8/10
Commentary:
This is the third version of urban and it still feels and plays like a fresh map. If you have played the previous versions, you will notice the same basic street layout, resembling a 'U'. The placement of some building and objects, such as ladders and fences which stay consistent through each 'new and improved' version. For example, the wooden fence and the A framed roof at the end of the street. There is much more roof access then before, so be ready to climb many ladders and jump onto many ledges. The flow of the games does stay consistent, like most maps of this kind it can be challenging to achieve that final kill. For obvious reasons, this is a sniper paradise. Many high roofs and areas of cover when drawing fire. The MP4 and machinegun are also choice weapons when it comes to weapon selection.

How It Looks score 6/10
Commentary:
Don't those same old building textures get old? Why yes they do, especially on this map. Your basic red brick and white/gray concrete make up 90% of the buildings. The only exception, a non-accessible, white pane building, tallest on the map. Gerbil!, we need some more variety in your textures for your upcoming maps. The sky, when viewed from ground level is clean. Another rendition of blue skies with white, wispy clouds. Yawn. There does seem to be a problem when viewing buildings from across the entire map. The build become intermittent, as pieces disappear. Possibly the map is too big? I don't know, I don't map. I does seem to be only in a few areas, on a few buildings.

Gameplay (fun factor) score 9/10
Commentary:
I am not sure what it is about his maps, but I enjoy teamplay on the urban series. As we know the city theme is old and reused, but it makes for good, solid game of team Action. Another in which snipers thoroughly enjoy. The map is extremely large and makes it difficult for a player to see the far reaches of the other side, giving the advantage to the sniper. There is a spot where a team spawns in a small room on the roof (pictured above). This has caused members to become stuck and easy targets for the other team. Also if you do not get off this roof, chances are you will be killed by a snipers bullet. Why? Because the other team spawn on building directly across from this spot making it difficult to descend off the roof if caught up there. Teamplay flows well on urban3

Ambiance score 5/10
Commentary:
Not much, since it is daytime. Although in the building pictured below, there is nice shadow cast by the light shinning down through the hole in the roof. But that is the extent of lighting effects.

Other:
What can you say? This is the third urban map in the series? The concept is old, but the map plays well, I guess there is where it makes up for being so unoriginal. Do these city maps ever get old? Yes they do, if they are done incorrectly (blood). This map, in my opinion is done right to give you a "new" feeling to an old theme.

Conclusion:
If you enjoyed playing the other two maps in the trilogy, then take the time to download this one. There is nothing new he has added. You will get the same feeling you had on the previous maps. Some of the buildings have been reworked, as have the ladders. I guess I have something for city maps, I like playing here, even though some of the scores do not reflect the playability of the map. Take it or leave it, nothing special about the new urban3

Overall score: 7/10
