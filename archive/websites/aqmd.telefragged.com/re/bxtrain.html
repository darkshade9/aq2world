Commentary:
This map is really well laid out, it's basicly four train cars running along a tunnel. This simple idea works so well, especially for teamplay where someone running along the top of the train can get past people and sneak up behind them. You gotta watch your back and use sound. There is a real feeling of movement on this map, it's really exhilirating.

Commentary:
The look on this map really grows on you. At first I thought the indoor textures were too bright, but they contrast with the outside ones which are much duller. I love the tunnel wall textures as well, just right for the moving feel. The darkness above the train is great as well, you catch glimpses of people running along.

You can use all of the weapons and succeed (yes, even the sniper rifle), and I haven't decided which is best. At first the handcannon was good, but the shotgun gave you more flexiblily. On the other hand, I just won a game with the M4....

Other:
I'd like you to note this is my first review, mainly because this map enspired me to do it. Otherwise, I think I've said all I need to.

Conclusion:
Basicly, this map is a blast. It's superb fun, and everyone should try it.

- Reviewed by Ronin

REVIEW

Architecture & Layout score = 9/10
Commentary:
= Heh, the layout on this map is pretty sweet. Though it is just a straight-away map, I liked the placement of objects and walls to slightly impede movement and force shooters to not sit back but to charge. Boxes are arranged to give cover, and the passenger section of the train allows one to duck between the seats and take adequate cover. The layout of the rooms allow for ambush points, as their doors are perpendicular to the hallways. The straight hallways makes lining up multiple targets easy.

How It Looks score = 9/10
Commentary:
Hell, I was stunned when I first saw this map. THE TRAIN WAS MOVING, or so it seemed. The mapper made the background of the map very realistic, the train was not moving, but the tunnel itself moved. The train actually looks like a train, with windows, ladders, seats, lounge area, and even a light at the front of the train. The interior color of the train was a little funky looking, a peachy orange color. Besides that, the looks on this baby will blow most Action players away.

Gameplay (fun factor) score = 9/10
Commentary:
Gameplay on this map is extremly close-quarters, except on the dangerous roof. Battles are short and sweet, as this is one of the smallest maps for Action Quake. I suggest close quarter weapons and kevlar for a map like this, for instance a shotgun with the body armor. Roof fighting is dangerous because on hit on you may knock you off the train onto the burning tracks:). The narrow hallways and tight quarters allows shooters to not be that good of shots and still hit. Nothin' better than someone coming around the corner right up to the barrel of your M4!

Ambiance score = 10/10
Commentary:
Heh, this map reminds me of a scarry anti-terrorist takedown of a train that is occupied with terrorists. One team starts at one end of the train, another team starts at the other side. Just like a mini-war, men shout commands to cover this door, and people sneak up on the roof on others. It creates a very action themed experience. The cars have to be cleared out one by one, and every car is an fight waiting to happen, nice job there BunnyX!

Other:
Teamplay was awesome; however, since that is pretty much all I play, I don't know much about the DM value of this map.

Conclusion:
I was itching to play this map, and today I did on an AR server. The experience was fast, one of the fastest maps on Action Quake. This means no waiting, and no camping. There is pretty much no where to hide nor no where to camp. Great job with this one!

Overall score: = 9/10

How It Looks score = 6/10
Commentary:
The textures are a bit misplaced, I think. They are crisp and clear but the box cars look more like barns on wheels than train cars. Its also pretty repetative.

Gameplay (fun factor) score = 5/10
Commentary:
I admit, this was pretty fun at first. But most maps are. The problem is that those long corridors begin to get on your nerves after awhile. I mean, there's basically one way to go.

Ambiance score = 10/10
Commentary:
Ambiance is where this map shines. No map before has so completely encaptured the feel of what it is like this one. I mean, this IS a train in a dark tunnel. The lighting is really nice;outside its dark with a few lights but nice and bright inside, with those lights shining through the windows against the tunnel walls.
