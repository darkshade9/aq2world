Review

Architecture Rating: 3/10
Architecture Comments:
This map reminds me of Blockwar, which is a bad thing. I think there are way too many places to hide and camp in. The architecture can be described as simplistic functionalism, which translates roughly as "where did you hide all the details?". On the bonus side, the layout does permit the use of all weapons, so no sprayer/sniper advantages here. All in all nothing really catches my eye with its brilliance and thus I can't give too many points for this one.

Looks Rating: 3/10
Looks Comments:
Simplistic and nondescript are the words for this one. Not particularly ugly, but not that pleasant either. I did not like the textures very much and the lack of notable detail does not add to the eye candy-meter either.

Gameplay Rating: 2/10
Gameplay Comments:
Boring. That's really all I can say. There's nothing here I haven't seen or experienced before and playing just wasn't fun at all. In addition the famous "search for the last living enemy"-scenario can last for a frustratingly long time. I was actually glad when the next map in rotation kicked in.

Ambiance Rating: 3/10
Ambiance Comments:
The feeling is as flat as the Netherlands. No fun or atmosphere here whatsoever. Actually I think this is a map equivalent of TV dinners. Devoid of taste, that is.

Other Comments:

Conclusion:
I can't recommend this map to anyone for the lack of fun kills the game efficently.

Overall Rating: 2

Reviewed By: Scinet .L.

REVIEW
Architecture & Layout score 9/10
Commentary:
The general layout of this map may be common, but it sure isin't boring to look at. The buildings have more then the average ammount of detail you usally find in a Action Quake2 map, the streets are large enough to have a good fight without having to worry about bumping into a wall. Sure, some of the textures look pretty misplaced or overused, but overall, the ammount of detail and the quality of the layout is well worth a 9/10.

How It Looks score 10/10
Commentary:
There's no doubt about this, Goose knows how to do lighting and texture choosing! The streetlights are by far the most realistic i've seen, producing a nice ammount of light that adds ALOT to the ambience of the map. The overall ambient lighting of the map is just awesome. It's well-balanced and it doesn't stick out in certain spots. It's not too bright or not too dark. Awesome!

Gameplay (fun factor) score 8/10
Commentary:
Can you say M4 plus laser sight? This level is perfect for both close-up attacks and long-range sniping. It's well balanced throughout the map with the rooftops, ladders and streets. The ammo and weapon placement was well thought of and is pefectly balanced. The one thing that can get annoying is if someone hides in one of the bedrooms in the hotel building. Rounds can then last forever untill someone finds the lamer.

Ambiance score 7/10
Commentary:
As noted above, the awesome lighting adds alot of ambience to the map. The main thing that's missing though is an appropriate ammount of ambient sounds. You need good ambient sounds to add ambiance! An ambiance freak will find this map an average map. A little more work could have been done on this.

Other:
The r_speeds of this map are awesome! Everyone has an equal chance of playing no matter their system configuration.

Conclusion:
This map is well worth the download, it has all the basic qualities you know and love in Action Quake2 maps...plus a little extras. I look forward to another great map from Goose!

- Reviewed by Violent Marv

REVIEW
Architecture & Layout score 6/10
Commentary:
I can only compare this map to blockwar, and, well, I loved blockwar. It's a city type setting, but the focus is more on being on the streets below the buildings as opposed to the more Urban style roof top fights. Although there are many insides of buildings and roof tops that are accessible, it is mainly a street fight. I would have given this segment a better mark, but the layout had one major flaw: many parts of the map were accessible only by the use of ladders, and the ladders were very poorly placed and tended to be only usable when heading up. Often I found myself climbing a ladder, reaching a roof top, wanting to get down, and dying miserably during the attempt because there was no other way down.

How It Looks score 7/10
Commentary:
The map is relatively plain in look, but it has a very nice colour scheme, sort of reddish purple, very dark like. It works well with the whole quake engine deal, and is never too bright or too dark. There are shadows about, but no one becomes completely lost in them, it works more in the way that someone is not seen for a second in the shadows, giving them enough time to shoot you before it's too late.

Gameplay (fun factor) score 7/10
Commentary:
The map was pretty fun, but I found all that ever happened is I'd turn a corner, be on the street, and someone would be high up on a ledge, and a lot of shots would be fired and someone would die (of course, this is how the game works). But it seemed that contact with others always occured at the same places, at least in teamplay, and if anyone decided to camp out in a building the round would go on forever.

Ambiance score 8/10
Commentary:
The colour scheme is really very attractive. It looks a hell of a lot nicer than say avenue..it has a similar colour, but a much nicer and darker red tone. The large truck is a nice addition, and is much better done than your average vehicle in a map (those limos lend to being very ugly).

Other:

Conclusion:
A good map, but those ladders really suck. I wish it was easier to climb down ladders, but it ain't. That's quake for ya. Either way, it's a good map, and worth playing, but gets a bit repetitive. Nothing too new either, I think I'll stick with block war.

Overall score: 7/10

- Reviewed by WarGod{WD}

REVIEW
Architecture & Layout score 8/10
Commentary:
Layout wise, there is very little to fault. The whole map is nicely accessible with only the warehouse being a potential hotspot for tp camping, otherwise the map manages to be spacious enough to allow for decent dm games while not having too many tiny rooms resulting in blockwar 1on1 syndrome. Outstanding, and a resonably original layout (that is, as original as an urban map can get now :)

How It Looks score 7/10
Commentary:
At first glance the whole map seems very simplistic with detail being used quite sparingly, but this is offset by the nice textures that goose has used throughout the map. Although this may sound quite negative, you quickly notice the gains associated with the lack of fine detail, even on my testing machine (p100/16meg/12meg vII) I noticed NO slowdown whatsoever in both gl and software modes. A certain other city style mapper would do well to take notes here.....

Gameplay (fun factor) score 9/10
Commentary:
Wooo a map that I enjoy dming on! The low brush counts help this map along nicely and all the tight jumps are nice and easy to manage so you dont repeatly end up divebombing onto the tarmac. (Ive lost count of all the llama shaped craters on urban1 :) Also this accessability encourages players to experiment running round the rooftops and ledges rather than camping behind doors with handcannons.

Ambiance score 5/10
Commentary:
The main falling here is the lack of fine detail, but otherwise the map is nicely lit with none of the fullbright/pitch black areas seen elsewhere, a bit of ambient sound wouldn't go amis tho.

Other:
My final point is that as good as a city map is, it will always be just another map lost in the endless crowd of urbans and cities etc.... Lets see something different yeah? :) Heck one day I might even get Uss Chimera done ;)

Conclusion:
This really is a good city map and deserves to be played on servers, the layout really does make it stand out from the majority of the crowd. Goose has resisted the temptation to simply make a box and fill it with other boxes :) Nice work.
