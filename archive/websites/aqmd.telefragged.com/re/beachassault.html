Reviewed By: Mr.Had3s

Review

Architecture Rating: 8/10
Architecture Comments:
Beachassault is one of the most original maps I have seen in a long time. The layout is great because it almost always ensures a stylish and flashy battle. Take a ride on the helicopter and take out a "Death from above" attack, which never fails to satisfy. The setup is basicly a fort on a beach, with a boat in the water. You either attack or defend the beach. A sweet trench system with lots of bunkers makes up the other side of the fort. A fort with climable walls into widows looks over the water where a platoon boat sits with a helicopter on top that will lift you into the base. These elements interact perfectly to give the player a flow to follow, making for a better game.

Looks Rating: 8/10
Looks Comments:
It is easy to see how much work went into this map. For someone with a good system, the effects on this map are great. Explosions rock the map, giving it a good wartime feel. The flashy textures are mostly custom, and they are used well. The architecture combined with these phat textures makes an impressive map. Just take a look at ANY part of the map and you will be impressed. On the flip side, somone with a slower system will have a hard time running this map, because of all the effects going down.

Gameplay Rating: 9/10
Gameplay Comments:
Never before have I played a map that affected the gameplay as much as this. The feeling is exilerating as you fly over the enemy in the helicopter pumping ammo into them. Also, a dual gets crazy quick as all of the sudden you have mines laying around that will send you flying halfway arcoss the map, into shark infested waters! Yes sharks that will bite you, three turrets that shoot and shooting someone out of the heli really kicks the llama's ass. :) If you don't like this map, I will be really suprised. Hell I had fun playing it by myself!

Ambiance Rating: 8/10
Ambiance Comments:
This map has constant sounds. Shelling, machine gun fire echo across the map all of the time. Although, some players with slower computers will find themselves a bit lagged by all this action, the effect is immense on gameplay. First of all, you have a perfect scene, with everything needed for a good war. Now with all these sounds, it gets hard to detect an attcack, because footsteps aren't as obvious. You feel like a soldier, and running across the beach an into the enemy is a feeling one won't forget. At times, you can even get a little scared. This map is definately creepy at times. Always a treat to play.

Other Comments:
Also, playing this map in AQ:E is really a treat. Especially with the scenarios that are coming out for it. It triples gamplay.

Conclusion:
Bottom line, this map is very interactive, and you should enjoy playing it. Players with slower computers may have a harder time, although it is still possible to play it smoothly most of the time.

Overall Rating: 8

Architecture Rating: /10 (Error in Review Form)
Architecture Comments:
Hey all!! The Rookie here with another AQ2 map review of the long awaited and much hyped Beachassault and quite frankly I have one question, why does everyone like this map? The maps layout is fairly simple. One side is a fort with manned guns, land mines and secret passages while the other is basically a giant boat featuring the now classic helicopter and sharks in the water. While a little deceiving this map is basically the classic TF "2fort" map in an ingenious disguise. Personally, I hate the "2fort" style for AQ2, especially for AQ2 TP. Since there's no real goal for defending your base then what's the point?

Looks Rating: 6/10
Looks Comments:
Lots of cool lighting effects in this map but texture wise it's kind of dry. These textures or similar ones have been seen before and probably with better usuage. The walls, ground, cliffs, boat, all a static grey, which is bland and boring.

Gameplay Rating: 4/10
Gameplay Comments:
Ugh!!! While oftend herald for it's "TP playability" Beachassault is one of the most boring TP experiences I've ever had. After the initial thrill of the cool effects you realise that none of them truly aid in TP and most pf them actually get you killed. DM is a different story, where it's a lot more fun but the openess of the map over all kills it. I always stress about the issue of cover in AQ2 maps but this map truly disregards it. There is cover, but ti's way too sparse, it needs a lot more. Add in the fact that you need to have a Pentium 500000 with 200 voodoo2's sli to get 20 FPS and you got one unfun experience.

Ambiance Rating: 10/10
Ambiance Comments:
This map is dark and loud, something I would suspect from this map who's theme is definitely "war." From hiding in the trenches to coming out of the boat the map truly feels very war like, it's almost like watching "Apocalypse Now" or "Saving Private Ryan."

Other Comments:

Conclusion:
Overall this map tried to do too much and ended up with nothing at all. There is a lot of originality in this map, but mappers really need to learn to work with, not against, Quake 2's limitations. Overall this map is laggy, slow and uninspired design wise which leaves a bad taste in your mouth like an big special effects with no plot. I myself will take gameplay over flash and effects anyday.
