Architecture Comments:
A small map with good vertical variance. Half the map is on high ground, half the map on low ground. Manuevering the cliffs can be tricky at first but after a while intermediate and advanced players will come up with clever ways of jumping from one place to another. There are numerous ways to get from one side of the map to another although not all are apparent the first few times you play this map.

Looks Rating: 6 /10

Fairly bright outdoor setting. A couple waterfalls to swim up or down but mostly just cliffs. Either side has a small building with a cable car that runs in between.

Gameplay Rating: 7 /10

This map is loads of fun. Automonkeys will be prevelant for sure but a shotgun (or hand cannon if you are a little daring) will do well also. This isn't the greatest sniping map but the layout doesn't stop the best snipers from making their presence felt. Ambiance Rating: 3 /10
Ambiance Comments:
Not much to say here. The waterfalls are a nice touch though.

Other Comments:
My only complaint about this map is the spawn point in the bottom most part of the map. There is only one way out - a ladder that is ridiculously easy for the opposing team to camp.

Conclusion:
Cursed by some, loved by others cliff2 is a lot of fun to play. Caters to any weapon and rounds are usually short and sweet, fast and furious.

Review:
CLIFF2 is a good example of a map that's one build away from excellence.

The Layout is very good and far less prone to the annoying fatal falls that Cliff 1 was. There are more nooks and crannies to manouver in, and for team-play the opportunies to flank the opposition will make for some great games.

It has low r_speeds on average, peaking at 600-700, but more often than not sitting around 400-550. With a GL Card this won't be a problem.

From an aesthetic point-of-view, however, there are some things I'll take issue with:

1 -- The Water. Yes, there's water and while the idea is good the execution is weak. The Map maker obviously hadn't had much experience modelling water before, since curret seems to be mising. What this means is instead of a river which pulls you down stream (and over the falls) you can swim UP the water fall. Also the transparency is set at 33, meaning the water is almost invisible. It looks sloppy. A shame since a few extra hours of testing and compiling time (a lot of functions like water and sun_light can be tested in smaller maps before being applied to the final BSP) and it could've worked well as an enhancement.

2 -- The lighting is flat and uninteresting. I'm not sure if he used the QRAD sun feature and dialed up the ambiance, or just lit it using sky, but the dramatic shadows and red rock texture of Cliff 1 are gone. Instead everything tends to be a vague shade of white light and almost shadowless.

This map may play just great (which is what really counts) but it could've looked and played better (which is a shame).

Review:
Let me first say--I primarily play teamplay, and so any map i play I look at from that perspective. However, this is a fun deathmatch level. Fast, not so huge that you *MUST* snipe, not so small that handcannons and shotguns rule the world. Smart play and a pistol can beat a M4 on this level, but it takes some work (as it should ;).

Now, lets move on to teamplay. Here, again, this map is fun. Fast, yes, and yes it does only take a few seconds to get to the other side. But, the layout is such that a couple of decent snipers can hold the base while the machine guns and shot guns fight for the middle. Additionally, the loved/hated choke points are less choked (but still there). You have four choices, two of which are so nasty they almost shouldn't be considered as options. First, you can go across with the cable, either in the trolly or on the cable itself. The reasons to not do this are obvious--go ahead and stand still in the open, you'll get the same results. Second, you can go across the ledge on the side (right side if you are on the side with the waterfall); however, this again has the same drawback--you will be in the open on a narrow path the entire way. You can cross on the bottom. This is fun--no matter how people complain, the ditch is a great place to fight in. Only the sniper rifle has a definite disadvantage--all other weapons are pretty equal if you know how to use the terrain. And while you are at a disadvantage with people firing from the top, you still have some cover and they have much more of their backs exposed than you do (if you are on a good team, this is something to be considered seriously). Finally, you can play 'king of the hill'. Usually, this is how all the M4s and shotguns die, is rushing maddly to get to the top before the other team does. Domination of the hill can translate to domination of the round--a slight weakness in the map, but not a fatal one if your snipers have cleared the other paths of players. Additionally, there is a slight advantage here for the team that climbs from the side with the water--they can enter in two directions without major hassle, and they can also walk off at two different spots. I suppose this makes up for the being fairly well trapped by the ditch on the other side (again, a weakness in the map, but can be worked around).

Finally, let me talk about the much maligned water on this level. Yes, it is light. Yes, you can go UP the waterfalls. So what? This level seems all about fast thinking and quick adaption to situation (which team controls the hill, which path do you take from this spawn point with your current weapon, etc). Why is this relevant? Because levels with opaque (or extremely close) water almost always find a few people who camp out in them, surfacing for air only when necessary, and waiting to hear footsteps. On this level, that would be lame in the extreme, and the see-through water prevents this. Play the map some, then answer this question--Do you really want someone able to camp unseen in either one of the ponds, or anywhere in the waterfall system? It may have been unintentional, but it is a good thing.

Same is true with the lack of currents. If you couldn't climb up the waterfall, the only way out of the ditch or lower pond would be up that ladder. After a few AP rounds to the head, most people would delete the map, quit playing AQ2, and take up golf full time (ok, maybe not the second two). Having the 'water ladder' provides a second (albeit also not very good) way of getting out of the bottom pond.

As for not falling as easily--GOOD! The original cliff was death to anyone with ploss (regardless of their ping) because the server would never get the info that you turned, or stopped moving forward, or jumped. Now, you have fewer places to fall at. The best thing related to falling in the original is still in place in this--you can still shoot people off a ledge and almost across the map. Happy thoughts truly aren't good enuff--you gotta teach those suckers how to fly. Enuff from me, I highly recommend this map. Bright, so no subway2 syndrome. Multiple paths to the bases, so no Desert Bases syndrome. Fairly simple and very open, so no 10 minute delays while 2 people try to find each other (ala Museum or Blockwar). Fast and furious, and any weapon can work.

Structure and Design: Great! All the areas of this map flow together perfectly. The author knew what he wanted this map to look like, right from the beginning. The waterfalls actually serve a purpose, either as a quick getaway or as a great sniping spot (trust me). The trolley is just as much fun as it was in the first cliff map. If you have balls of steel, go ahead and ride it. Although it is alot of fun to pepper the entire trolley with machine gun fire and see the person inside jump to his doom. Great design.

Weapons Placement: Beats me. The only way I have played this map is through teamplay. Judging on the quality of the map though. There is probably great weapons placement.

Gameplay: Excellent! Some maps get old real quick, but this one just gets better and better. There is so much strategy to use on this map, it will never get old. Use the sniper rifle, mp5, m4, shotgun, or handcannon. You can have success with every weapon if you know how to use them. One of the most enjoyable maps to play. Never gets boring.

Framerates: Extremely good. I have a Pentium 200 with a Monster 3D card. No slow down at all. I didn't check to see what it was like without hardware acceleration, but in the future I will.

How it looks: This is such a beautiful map. I love maps that are bright and well lit. I've seen so many maps with good design wasted by being too dark. Eye candy.

Overall:
Structure & Design: 9/10
Weapons Placement: ?
Gameplay: 9/10
Framerates: 9/10
Looks: 10/10

Overall score: 9/10

Great looking map, mappers should use this map as an example of how a great map make Action Quake2 kick serious ass!
