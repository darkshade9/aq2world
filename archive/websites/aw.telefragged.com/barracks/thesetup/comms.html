Ok that covers all the radio calls, but there is still alot more you can do to get the most out of your radio. Like adding personalized messages into your radio calls, so instead of just saying "team mate down" you can say "Team mate down, man down! man down! Send backup!" or whatever you want. So how do you do this? Simple here's how the message above should look in your console:

Bind x "radio teamdown;say_team Man down! Man down! Send backup!"

Just replace with any key you wish and bobs ya uncle!

You can do these to any radio command you wan't, just remember to include say_team because that means that it will only send it to the people in your team.

Ok so what good is saying "cover me" when no one knows who you are asking? Or saying im hit without saying how much health you have? Or even reporting in and not saying your weapon and ammo? Well squaddies there is a way to do the and it the commands are called

Variables
These are nifty little commands that can show your status on multiple things for your team mates to know, like current health, ammo, weapon, who is near you etc.
Here is a list of all the variables in AQ2:
  • %H - This tells your team mates/partner your current health. VERY handy.
  • %A - This tells your team mates/partner your current ammo, and remaining clips.
  • %T - This tells your team mates/partner the names of the teammates nearest to you.
  • %W - This will tell your team mates/patner your current weapon
  • %I - This will tell your team mates/partner your current item
 These variables allow you to make much more detialed messages, like for example if the main heat of the battle is over and you dont know who is left on your team and then suddenly a voice blurts out "team repot in" instead of you just replying "reporting in" you can tell him your health, weapon, ammo and item. And how do you do this you ask? Simple just combine what we have already learnt and chuck in the variable so it would look something like this:

"radio reportin;say_team reporting in with %H health a %W with %A and a %I"

Which would say:

"Reporting in with 68 health a M4 assult rifle with 16 bullets and 1 extra clip and a kevlar vest"

So you can see how we have transformed a normal boring radio call into a work of art! Well not really but close :)
