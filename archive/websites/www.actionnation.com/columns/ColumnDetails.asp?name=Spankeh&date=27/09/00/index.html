CS Survival Guide Part 1 22 November 2000

I dont think anyone was really ready for the phenomenon that was to become Counterstrike. Here we are many beta releases later emerging into what looks like a commercial release for Valve and the CS team. From its humble beginnings, this simple mod for HL has rose into the most played online game currently in the world.

Of course, with any popular mod comes a community that builds itself around it, and CS is no exception. Over the next month or so, in this exclusive set of licensed survival guides (tm), I intend to try and guide the folly amongst you, or the weak of faith into becoming a fully fledged and incorporated member of the CS fraternity.

In this first part of a serialisation I intend to delve into the secrets of IRC, the popular chatting device that brigns so much joy to so many, so without further ado, here is the official guide to IRC in Counterstrike.

Getting Started : Joining a sutible Counterstrike channel is of the first priority, each offering different, yet always engaging levels of maturity. You could do a lot worse than beginning your journey in the Premiere Uk chat scene, #Cstrike.uk . As you enter and say hi, do not be put off by the silence, or the conversation that does not encompass you. This is merely the average CS players way of greeting you, so as a reult, you should immediately feel at home. And anyway with your superb name of l33tM45t3R, what do you have to be afraid of?

Talking in #CStrike: By now you will have also learnt to say nothing when someone joins, or simply carry on in your conversation to make them feel at home... but the conversation... ahhh!, How do you type in the sacred place? As an example, let us consider the quite reasonable sentence that affects most CS players:

Sigh, im not all that good at the game of CS and i have a ping of 270. It`s really difficult to stay alive a lot of the time.

Nononoo, thats not the way to do it on IRC, you must convert your text into IRC understandable prose. Firstly, it is imperative to do away with all punctuation, this gives the impression you are hurried, and agitated about your current state, thus:

sigh im not all that good at the game of cs and i have a ping of 270 its really difficult to stay alive a lot of the time

Next, change all letter in the sentence to capitals. This demands attention as your text reads out over the surrounding masses. Repeat it 2 or 3 times also to make sure everyone hears it and to guarentee as answer.

SIGH IM NOT ALL THAT GOOD AT THE GAME OF CS AND I HAVE A PING OF 270 ITS REALLY DIFFICULT TO STAY ALIVE A LOT OF THE TIME

SIGH IM NOT ALL THAT GOOD AT THE GAME OF CS AND I HAVE A PING OF 270 ITS REALLY DIFFICULT TO STAY ALIVE A LOT OF THE TIME

SIGH IM NOT ALL THAT GOOD AT THE GAME OF CS AND I HAVE A PING OF 270 ITS REALLY DIFFICULT TO STAY ALIVE A LOT OF THE TIME

it is at this point however you realise that this takes quite a while to type out, and given perhaps the average intellegnce of the channel you are in may not be high enough to comprehend the full glory of your sentence, it is perhaps time to make it shorter, more snappy. Like so:

IM SHIT AT CS AND MY PING SUCKS

Lets not forget to repeat it several times now...

IM SHIT AT CS AND MY PING SUCKS
IM SHIT AT CS AND MY PING SUCKS
IM SHIT AT CS AND MY PING SUCKS

This is starting to get more to the point, a nice short snappy sentence that all can understand. But no. Still the masses look upon your english with distrust and confusion. Whatever have you done wrong now. Perhaps you arent accentuating your prose enough. That is part of it, add the following exclamation marks...

IM SHIT AT CS AND MY PING SUCKS!!!!

The thing is though, now youve been typing this for several minutes, your talent has had time to grow. You now realise... hang on, im not actually shit at CS any more. There is only one other reason that you could have possibly died.. Yep. They are CHEATING! Amend you sentence thus:

THEY CHEATED!!!! AND MY PING SUCKS!!!!

now come on, you have obviously been done out of what was rightly going to be a fair win for your self by a severe margin. Your not going to merely say "they cheated". Its time for the expletives. Fortunately, a shorthand for the term "for fucks sake" exists in the paraphase: FFS, hence:

FFS!! CHEAT! AND MY PING SUCKS!!!!

Your urgency is now starting to register on the Cstrike.uk massive. You may get a "eh?" appearing on your screen. Lets try saying it several times, see if theres any response:

FFS!! CHEAT! AND MY PING SUCKS!!!!
FFS!! CHEAT! AND MY PING SUCKS!!!!
FFS!! CHEAT! AND MY PING SUCKS!!!!

Not bad. A glimmer of interest from the residents. But your sentence lacks charisma, something to make it stand out above the crowd, some panache, a witty comment that would be at home on some up and coming C4 comedy show. What can you do. Change some letters in the sentence, that will show creativity! While your at it, how about some Ascii speak you learnt from a website and some more expletives

FFS!! CH3ET!!! FFS FUCKING PING SUX5!!!
FFS!! CH3ET!!! FFS FUCKING PING SUX5!!!
FFS!! CH3ET!!! FFS FUCKING PING SUX5!!!
Finally with the goal in site, you lose all control in your dramatic attempts to convince the posse that you need help. Desperate help. Add the final touches to your sentence:

FFS!11!! CHEE£T!1!! FUCKING RUSHING HEADSHOT PING SUCKS!11! YOU SUK!11
FFS!11!! CHEE£T!1!! FUCKING RUSHING HEADSHOT PING SUCKS!11! YOU SUK!11
FFS!11!! CHEE£T!1!! FUCKING RUSHING HEADSHOT PING SUCKS!11! YOU SUK!11
