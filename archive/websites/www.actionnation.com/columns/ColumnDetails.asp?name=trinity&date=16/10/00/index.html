A Theory 10 January 2001

Many of you may never have considered arguments of this nature but I have been considering this for weeks so I thought I`d share my thoughts.

The Problem

In the Bible it says that God has every day of everyone`s life written down in some type of celestial book. God also tells us that he has given us free will to choose to do what ever we want to do.
Therein lies an obvious paradox. How can God know what we are going to do in advance and yet still give us the choice in doing it?

My Solution

Consider this:
At any given moment we can make a conscious descision to do something (take your descision to read this column for example).
I suggest that whenever we make that descision God "redraws" the world as it were, with all of history in the background.
We go through life with the world begin redrawn after every descision up until we make our last conscious descision.
At this point we die, the current "frame" of time being active.
All through life we have made our own descisions and in that final frame God had our entire lives written in his "book" at the beginning of that "frame".
And thus, we made our own descisions, yet God had our lives written in his book all along.

If you would like me to explain this further then drop me a note. At home or at work.
