AQ:Tournament Edition
Review

The Tournament Edition is a joint venture between AQDT, The makers of AQ:Espionage and the AQ:G Team, makers of Gangsters. The idea is to make a mod for clanmatches, where Clanleaders will have control over the settings as well as making it harder to cheat.

No weapons or items has been changed from version 1.51. This is purely a mod to simplify Clanmatches. This doesn't mean that there aren't any new stuff added, clanleaders will have some reading to do, especially since it is now the official OGL-mod for the actionladder. There are 2 new "special" kind of players. The first one is the teamcaptain. Each team has a captain that can control certain serversettings that used to require rcon. The round can not begin until both captains has declared themselves ready and the settings will be displayed again before the round begins so that no one will sneak back and change the settings (not that any of you would cheat). So what awesome new powers does these captains possess?

I'll get back to this in the commands section, there's that other player which isn't even a player but whose power is even greater than that of the captains: The referee. The referee can single-handedly change the matchsettings once the captains has agreed on a referee, in fact, the captains can't change them once a referee has been appointed, they can however still change the teamname and models/skins. OK, enough of that, what has been changed for all of you that aren't a captain or referee? Quite a lot of stuff actually, most of it is transparent but there are some nice features:

The ability to pause between rounds, if one of the captains or the referee types "ready in the console during a round the next round will be paused until they are all ready again.

Limited chasecaming, The chasecam can be limited so that you only can follow your teammates.

You're still on a team even if you're not playing, this means that you can join with the entire clan and switch players in between rounds - with a simple 3 letter command "sub".

Real playtime, the clock is only ticking when you're playing.

And even more features in the commands section:

 
Commands

The captains' commands:

[Captain] - makes you the captain of your team, or if you're already captain of the team it will make you leave the post.

[Vote {mapname}] - (Captains only) votes on the map.

[Votelist] - displays the percentage of votes each map has received as well as the maps you can vote for.

[Referee {name}] - (Captains only) votes for a referee.

[Ready] - tells the server you're ready and starts the countdown for the round to begin. If you're already in a readystate it will unready you. Both captains and the referee has to be ready in order for the countdown to begin.

[Sub] - Switches between playing and being a substitute

[Teamname {name of team}] - (Captains only) Changes the name of your team.

[Teamskin {model/skin}] - (Captains only) Changes the model and skin of your team.

[Matchtime] - Displays the elapsed time

[silence {name}] - (Captains only) prevents a player from using messagemod1, a captain can only use it on his own team but the referee can silence anyone.

[Settings] This is where it really starts to get interesting, This is what lets the captains and referee change the serversettings to their liking. The command will bring up a menu containing the following commands:

* roundlimit
* timelimit
* roundtimelimit
* fraglimit
* maxplayers
* spectators allowed / not allowed
* messagemode1 enable/disable
* chasecam mode (Normal, Team Only, Team Eyes Only)
* tgren (0, 1, 2, 3)
* irvision on/off
* display score enable/disable

A more extensive list can be found in the README over at fear.
