CGF Features

Below, you'll find the list of AQ2CGF features, as they are to appear in the upcoming versions. I rather present you the features instead of stating that CGF has "incredible AI". That's up to you to decide, isn't.

Features in white have already been developed, whereas the features listed in grey are yet to be implemented in the corresponding version [last update: August 07, 99].
The list of features is broken down into four categories (for clarity - yeah right): Bots, Team Tactics and Tactical Terrain Understanding, Missions, or Scripting, maps, documentation, misc..
Version 0.8 will be the first public version, version 0.9 the next, etc.
And yes, the list is long because that's what it takes to play Action Quake2 :)

CGF 0.8 ('1st release') features

Bots
  • Vision affected by target distance, light level + muzzle flashes at target, ir vision, target movement, visible target body parts, target near border of field of vision, and glass between observer and target.
  • Hearing distinguishes between threat sounds (weapon sounds, steps, pain, etc.) and indirect sounds (doors opening or closing).
  • Reasonable (and not perfect) handling of AQ2 map items such as drain pipes/rope ladders, ordinary ladders, doors, as well as Coca Cola jumps (urban).
  • Rudimentary prevention of AQ2 falling damaging.
  • Bandaging.
  • Reloading.
  • Distance/ammo based weapon selection.
  • Use automatics (M4, MP5) in burst rate controlled mode and full automatic mode.
  • Use sniper rifle in standard and zoomed mode.
  • Use MK23 pistol and akimbo option.
  • Aiming accuracy governed by distance, target movement with regard to viewer, target tracking time, target visibility (see above) and skill.
  • Prioritize threats based on distance and weapon used.
  • Predict threat position based on last observed position, movement, combined with sound cues from threat.
  • Provide fire suppression on likely threat movement avenues, when applicable.
  • AQ2 combat radio usage.
Team Tactics and Tactical Terrain Understanding
  • Fire teams (size 1...8 members).
  • Dynamic combat situation assessment (using fuzzy set theory) determines most appropriate team tactics.
  • Standard team combat tactics: mass fire, reinforce front, pursuit, showdown/take cover, regroup.
  • Six team combat modes: attack, defend, advance, pullback, snipe/snipe & spot, patrol.
  • Dedicated AI for bounding overwatch move up (aka "advance") and pullback.
  • Dedicated AI for sniper and combined sniper/spotter operations.
  • Dedicated AI for patrolling the key areas of a map (areas listed in the script, teams can either patrol all areas or a per team defined subset).
  • Intra-team threat info sharing (team members respond to threats spotted and signalled by team mates).
  • Scriptable inter-team communication via go-codes.
  • Player to team-interaction via go-codes.
  • Understands terrain (cover positions, sniper positions (+ spotter positions), strong defensive positions, wall hugging positions).
  • Construct 'safe' and 'stealth' paths under combat (balancing distance and cover/wall hugging resp. out of sight/dark spots).
  • Standard (assembly) formations (line, wedge, star).
  • Path algorithms (Floyd, A*) and terrain algorithms (custom) use a pre-computed terrain database (<6MByte per map) where possible, enabling CGF to perform on lower-end CPUs
Missions
  • Mission file format is text (any text editor can be used to edit missions)
  • Missions define mission objectives, map, respawn rules, forces, teams, members + load out (= weapons, ammo, items), and initial team battle orders.
  • Two or more opposing forces.
  • Multiple teams per force (CPU bound).
  • Team contains up to 8 members and has initial battle orders.
  • Each team member may be customized (model & skin, weapons, ammo, items and skill [for bots]).
  • Force based scoring (instead of skin/model based).
  • Objectives include elimination of force, team, or individual, time out, and occupation of location.
  • Objective based mission branching (next mission is selected on objectives achieved/failed).
  • Per objective, multiple candidate next missions may be listed, from which a random mission is picked.
  • Team battle orders include commands such as insert, wait, moveup, attack, defend, radio, pullback, set on-contact behavior, spread out, and rudimentary (goto) control flow.
  • Stand-alone (command-line) mission verifier to support mission editing.
Scripting, Maps, Documentation, Misc.
  • Maps restricted to urban, city, downtown, (museum?) (route file creation for arbitrary maps not documented).
  • Training missions against fixed positions (team occupying certain locations).
  • Training missions based on specific weapon (sniper against patrolling teams)
  • Random team-on-team fight missions.
  • Basic script modification manual ('from scratch' script authoring not documented).
  • Multi-map chained missions (single player) and co-op multi-player.
  • Breakable glass

CGF 0.9 ('2nd release') features

Bots
  • Shotgun type weapons (M9 & Handcannon) and close combat AI.
  • Hand grenade usage.
  • Use of dropped weapons.
  • Misc. AI tuning and tweaks.
Team Tactics and Tactical Terrain Understanding
  • Terrain traffic analysis (learn the most visited and occupied terrain positions while playing and use that in the combat AI).
  • Misc. AI tuning and tweaks.
Missions
  • More robust mission execution.
  • Scripting fully documented.
Scripting, Maps, Documentation, Misc.
  • Support a couple of additional maps.

CGF 1.0 ('full version') features

Bots
  • Bot swimming.
  • Knives and jump-kicks.
  • Handles blocking (by force members).
  • Misc. AI tuning and tweaks.
Team Tactics and Tactical Terrain Understanding
  • Effective under friendly fire rules.
  • Misc. AI tuning and tweaks.
Missions
  • Additional respawn rules
Scripting, Maps, Documentation, Misc.
  • Support for arbitrary maps.
  • Route file creation documented.
