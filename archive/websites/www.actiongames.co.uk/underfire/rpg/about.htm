Under Fire: Massively Multiplayer

Well the above really says it all...well not exactly. Ok, this is basically an intro to the RPG game without the rules, so you can get a feel of what this site is and what we do here. Well let's begin at the begginning.

Basically, I am sick of having to monitor my online rpg's constantly, and most of my online rpg's have failed. But I tried over and over, to see all of them fail. So what I did instead of taking a new idea and just making a regular RPG for it I decided to step back and look at what screwed up all the other rpgs. The main thing that ended up ruining all my other rpgs was the fact no one could make it to chat rooms at the same time. Then I tried to fix that with message boards, but that was way to much work for me to do, and I had to limit the amount of members, and alot of members didn't participate. So a while back I thought, what if I could create the setting and let them do all of my work? haha, no way. But then I was talking to someone on ICQ and I figured it out.

Basically what you do is make your own webpage that has your character sheet on it and instead of me doing all the work on your character info, you do it. Then there are message boards, many of them. There are 5 boards for each topic. The reason for 5 boards per topic is for levels, the higher experienced people get to go to better message boards, where they can do more things.

So then there is the two main boards, the story boards and the battle boards, the names pretty much explains them all. If you want to work with the story you go to the story board of the level your character is at, you roleplay with other people and you can use everyone's characters in the story, but the rules and regulations of how you can use other people's characters are explained more in the rules.

Another thing different about this kind of RPG is you can just jump in at any time that you want to. All you need to do is go to the rules, read them over and create a webpage for your character, something as simple or elaborate as you want it to be. Once you have this page done you go to the story board, see what's going on and hop in, adding your personal touch to the story.

That's pretty much it for the intro of Under Fire, now time to go to the rules so you can learn how to play!
