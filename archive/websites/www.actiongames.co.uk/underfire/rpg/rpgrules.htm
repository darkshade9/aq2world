Too Complicated for About:

Ok, there is something I didn't mention in the about section but is necessary to mention before the rest is who exactly you are. Well there are sides, I decided to leave them open ended and blurred the lines because this isn't my RPG, it's yours. You also don't pick a side to be on, you make friends, make enemies, and make friends enemies. Who knows, through out the course of the RPG you and everyone else may think that your side is the good guys, but then I go to the message board, spice up the story a bit and now you are sort of wondering if you are really fighting for what's right.

As I mentioned before, I play many characters, and I also play god. I warn you, I will post things that will confuse you, but trust me, they will make much, much more sense later.

Section #1: How to create your character

Ok, so by now you have read the about section and gotten the background so you know a little bit about this RPG so thanks for coming here because that means you have gone from curious to interested, so basically that makes the purpose of this section to get you from interested to player. So once again, let's begin at the beginning.

The first thing you need to do is pick a character class, now the great thing about online real RPG's instead of Ultima or Everquest is the fact you aren't limited as much, so your character class isn't called character class anymore but rather your job. So that is the first real thing that you will need to put on your character sheet. Oh, by the way, you can check an example here. So as you can see the job is only proceeded by your characters name, sex, weight, height and age. All characters must start out under the age of 20, that could be 19 or it could be 1, but honestly, it could be pretty boring playing a one year old.

That brings up an issue, what's the point in a characters age height and weight, well basically I don't like getting all deep into detail like weight modifiers and stuff, it detracts from the story and experience, which is what makes an RPG an RPG. So basically, like alot of other things in this RPG, it is based on the honor system. For example, do you think that a 500 pound man could leap a 15 foot fence? Probably not on this planet. So think logically when you choose these options.

Master Skill

Ok, next comes master skill. Master Skill is the thing that your character does best. Everyone has all the skills listed below in some form, but if you have one of these as your Master Skill you rarely fail at it unless someone else has their master skill something that can counter your master skill. More on countering below. Your master skills can be:

Infiltration- If you have this skill you can sneak places easily. The only way you won't be able to do this is if, for example, post a message on the story board saying that you sneak into a hotel, but someone who is against you could respond to that saying that their master skill is Intelligence, then you have been countered. More on countering below.

Close Combat- Close Combat is a good skill for you if you like to get into fights alot in the game. If you like to do things with your body (not that way perv) like karate, and other forms of hand to hand combat this is for you.

General Weapons- General Weapons gives you basic knowledge of all major weapons except sniper rifle, and how to use them. You can use pistols, machine guns and other guns to use.

Sniper- Sniper is a rare trait because the only people that can hold a sniper rifle are the people that have it as their master skill, you may not hold or use a sniper rifle if it is not your main skill.

Demolitions- If you have this skill you can use bombs whenever. Of course, bombs come with consequences, accidents and people stopping them, but you can blow things up easily, this is one where creativity from you and the rest of the players really can make the game exciting.

Intelligence- Ok, let's get this off the plate right away: you don't use weapons. That's right, you don't really get to use them at all, if intelligence is your master skill you get intelligence and a bit of close combat. That doesn't mean that your character is weak, though, the thing that makes your character one of the, if not the, most crucial part to the story, is that, in one way or another you can counter every single other major skill out there, or get the opportunity to counter everyone. You can disarm bombs, find out where snipers are, use security cameras and other smart methods to find out if someone is infiltrating a building. But that isn't all, besides countering you could help people, by doing things such as telling your friends where snipers are, supply your buddies with weapons and other things to help out their missions or help one of your infiltrators by hacking into the system and watching the security cameras to alert him of any approaching security guards or other enemies. So as you can see, if you are an Intelligence guy, you may not be in there on the action but you can sure help alot of people out.

Master Skill Counters

Ok, the point of a master skill is you know it so well that you rarely do something wrong, but this wouldn't be a very fun game if you could do your master skill all the time. I like to force creativity, and counters does just that. I am going in a few seconds, give you the guidelines, but it is what you do with these guidelines that makes the counters so much fun to use. Below I will list the Master Skills and what other Master Skill can counter it. Here is the list of Counters

Master Skill

Master Skill(s) that can counter

Infiltration

Intelligence

Close Combat

Sniper

General Weapons

Close Combat

Sniper

Intelligence, Infiltration, General Weapons

Demolitions

Intelligence, Infiltration

Intelligence

General Weapons, Infiltration

Ok, now those are the counters. I guess that's it on master counters. Well I will try to go a little bit more in depth. Here's a little example:

Lets say that your master skill is infiltration. You are trying to sneak into a building. There is one guy on the story message board that doesn't want you to have this succeed for some reason. His Master Skill is Intelligence. He could make up a story about how his character was in the building's security system room. He sees the Infiltrator on the security camera. Now the Intelligence guy could get some of his friends to go into combat with the infiltrator.

Now that was an extremely simplified scenario, that could go for 25 threads about how the Intelligence guy had trouble getting into the building and lost a little health, but this brings up a topic:

Master-Counter Theorem #1: If someone makes something happen and the thing they made happen wasn't brought up before it is engraved in history and cannot be changed.

Corollary: The person that makes this event happen cannot bring up things to help his characters stats, only story related things.

So what this means is once the Intelligence Guy got to the security camera place, no body can master-counter that action. The character should not be punished for making a creative action that adds to the story. But also they cannot reward themselves for this. (e.g. On my way to the security room I found 24 AK-47's and am handing them out to so and so) That can't happen. But if a person does come up with a good idea they could very well be rewarded by the board moderator.

NEW TERM- MODERATOR: The "gamemasters" or "dungeonmasters" (for our AD&D friends) of this RPG. They are people appointed by only myself and have the power to help characters out and add plot twists. As of writing this I am the only one and have no immediate intentions of getting another moderator, when the game becomes big enough or I can't do this anymore. I don't want anyone asking me to be a Moderator.

Normal Skills

Every Person has one thing they excel at but that does not mean at all that people can't do anything else. That's why you have a little of everything, except for sniper, sniper is a skill that you only have if it is your master skill...as mentioned earlier. You get 12 initial points to split up among your normal skills.

NEW TERM- Points: If you have played a normal RPG before you can think of these points as "d" or dice that you would split up with your abilities. You can earn more in the future by gaining experience points or from the moderator. Points play a big hand in battle.

NEW TERM- Experience Points: Odds are that no matter what kind of RPG you have ever played they have had Experience points. Basically when you earn enough experience points you get something more real, in this case points.

Splitting up points: It's quite easy really. You have 1 Master Skill, then since you can't have any bit of a sniper skill that makes 4 skills left for you to be your normal skills.

NEW FORMULA: 6-1-1=4
6 (original skills) - 1 (Master Skill) - 1 (Sniper skill) = 4 (skills left)

You could choose to split up your points evenly (3 points to each skill) or divide them up based on how much you think they are important.

Special Rule For Snipers: Scince you do have the sniper skill you get 5 normal skills and 15 starting points, but you must have a maximum of 3 points on at least 2 of your normal skills.

These Points will be what makes and breaks your character in battle and other situations, but how to apply these will be explained later.

Experience Points

Next on the sample character sheet comes experience points. Each of your normal skills has it's own experience points category. The points to distribute are given to you through several ways, the most common being these:

Moderator: If you do a great thing on the story board then the moderator could give any number of them to you. When he gives them to you he should specify whether or not it is for a specific skill or for you to put in any skill.

Battle: You can take experience points from the person you battle. At the end of the battle the winner takes the number of threads the battle took times the number of points the skill that you want to get experience points in.

NEW FORMULA: T x S = E
T (# of threads in the battle) x S (# points of the skill of your choice) = E (winners gained experience points, loser's lost experience points.)

Example: Let's say you win a battle and think you need more points in your General Weapons skill. You currently have 2 points in your General Weapons category. The battle you just won took 10 threads (messages on the message board for your battle) to complete. According to the formula you would take 2 times 10. That gives you 20 experience points for your General Weapons experience points. This also subtracts 20 experience points from your opponent. This can be all from one skill's experience points or split up among different skills' experience points. It doesn't really matter, as long as your opponent looses 20 experience points. Also, once you have won the battle you need to show your opponent the formula, like this:

T=10
S=General Weapons=2
10x2=20

That's all you would need to do and it would be ok. This shows your opponent you aren't cheating and tells him how many points he needs to loose.

Getting Points with Experience points: Ok so you have battled and battled and worked and worked on the boards and want to cash in. Simple, once you have accumulated 100 experience points in a skill experience points category you can add one point to that skill.

New Formula: 100E=1P
100E(experience points of a skill)=1P(point for that skill)
(I don't want any critisizing on this equation as it is here just to help you out)

Action Points:

Action Points are basically signs that you are human. You can't go forever without wearing out. They are also the driving force of battle, everything relies on AP. You get 40 AP before every battle automatically and after you go straight to 40 and maybe a bonus (which we will talk about later.) When you first fill out your character page put 40 here.

Section #2: Battling

I'll bet that you have been wondering how this was going to be handled from the very start, didn't you? Well so was I. This has been one of the biggest obstacles, but I think I got it down. When you find out what the battle system is you will probably think "wow, that's stupid, it's just so simple" but basically, that's what I was aiming for. As I have mentioned already, I am aiming for story and a fun game, not a story that has to be bogged down by tons of complicated rules.

When it comes to the battle system I have one controversial thing to say that you haven't heard me say anywhere else: Characters don't die in battles. The only place a character can die is on the story board and the only person that can kill a character is the person that controls that character. Basically what that means is when you are sick of the game you come up with a way for your character to die and put it in the story board.

Starting the Battle: This is the easiest part, you post a message on the Story Board with the subject being Battle Challenge. You find some way to relate the story to you challanging someone to a battle. You can make this a direct challange to a specific character or open ended. Or request an NPC battle. If you want an NPC battle you must specify what NPC you want to battle. One way or another, someone has to respond to your post before you start the battle on the battle board. The response doesn't need to be much, it could be a simple: I accept, or it could be a more intricate way of saying I accept. The purpose of the response is to let the person who started the battle know that you know about the battle.

The Big Question: Then you are wondering, if you can't die in battles, then how do battles end? Well the first person to run out of AP is the looser. Nuff said. Well not quite enough. Ok, remember that Master Counter thing from a while back? Well you know what I'm talking about but don't know it exactly, well here it is again:

Normal Skill

Normal Skill(s) that can counter

Infiltration

Intelligence, General Weapons

Close Combat

General Weapons, Close Combat

General Weapons

Close Combat, General Weapons

Demolitions

Intelligence, Infiltration

Intelligence

General Weapons, Infiltration, Close Combat

Notice several things about this table, 1, instead of Master Skill and Master Skill(s)... headings it is Normal Skill and Normal Skill(s)... headings, and 2, there is no Sniper here. Why? Because as mentioned before, Sniper is only a Master skill, so, hence can only be used on the story board.

Starting the Fighting

The person who did the challange gets to set up the battle however he or she likes. The person says the intro: e.g. how they got to where they are, then states what he is doing and what skill they are using to do this. Then they state how many of their 40 AP they are using on this skill. When you use AP the number of AP used is subtracted from your AP You can use anywhere from 2-4 AP per turn.

Steps for Battle:
1. Background- how you and opponent got where you are.
2. State what you are going to do, in detail preferably.
3. State what skill you are using to do this.
4. State how many AP you are using on this skill. (2-4)
5. State how much AP you and your Opponent have left

NEW FORMULA: The battle formula is: A x S = D
A (# of AP you are using) x S (Points you have on Skill being used) = D (Damage on your opponent)

NEW TERM- Damage: Damage is simple, really all it is is the number of AP that your opponent needs to subtract from his AP.

Your Opponents Turn: Now that you have gone, it's your opponent's turn. Your opponent does the exact same steps that you did except is more limited. Your opponent needs to react on your action, by using the table above. So instead of deciding what skill your opponent would prefer to use, your opponent needs to go by the counters above. Battle Step 2 is really important, your opponent and you need to have a logical explaination for using the counter. You can't just say "I am using the General Weapons skill because the table says I can counter with it." You need it to make sense. Give an in depth story at how you did this, the more detailed you are the less unneccesary aurguments there are about "if that was legal or not."

This process goes over and over until one person is out of AP points, symbolizing that they are too tired to go on and you are nice enough to let him go, but not without stealing a few experience points as mentioned earlier and getting an AP bonus.

AP Bonus: If you win a battle, take the remaining AP you have and add 40, that is your next battles AP. Your opponent just adds 40.

NEW FORMULA: R+40=N
R(remaining AP) +40(automatic AP) = N (Next battles AP)

NPC Battles
NPC Battles are handled just like any other battle except when you challange an NPC anyone can fight using the NPC's stats. All NPC's and their stats are posted in the NPC's section of the page.

If the NPC looses this in no way affects the person controlling the NPC's character. It's just practice battles. If the NPC wins, however, then the NPC's stats are handled just as any other character's stats would be handled.

NOTE: If any NPC battles are rigged or set up just to improve stats and it is obvious there will be serious consequences.

Section #3: The Boards

The Boards are what makes the RPG. Without the boards there is nothing. There are 2 Boards- The Story Board and The Battle Board. I think they obviously give themselves away. I have said pretty much every detail you will need to know about the Battle Board, but I haven't been to specific about the Story Board. Well, the Story Board is where the heart and soul of the RPG is. You decide what your characters will do there and how they do the stuff. (Master Skills are for the Story Board only)

Using Normal Skills on the Story Board

You may also use your Normal Skills on the story Board. They are handled the exact same way as Master Skills, except you don't automatically get to do the thing you want to do. Before I explain any further here's that table again:

Intelligence, General Weapons

General Weapons

Now let's say you have 4 points on your Normal Skill Infiltration and you are trying to sneak somewhere, though infiltration isn't your master skill. Someone can counter you if they have an Intelligence or General Weapons Normal Skill with more than 4 points. It's that simple.

So that's how you do stuff in the story board, these are the only guidelines you have, the rules above and the Master Skill stuff, the rest is up to your creative ability.

Using other Player Characters in your Roleplays
You may use someone else in your roleplays on the story board, but you may make any decisions on behalf of that character. Let's put it this way, by the end of your roleplay involving another character the other character(s) you used should be exactly the same as they were before you roleplayed. Your character on the other hand...

Using NPC's in Roleplays

NPC's are crucial to the game, feel free to make up a character that your character encounters in the game. You can make this character have just about any background you feel would help the story. Also be aware when you create an NPC all other players in the game can use your NPC that you created for whatever they want, but then you can use that NPC for whatever you want also, as you can see this can lead to alot of plot twists, depending upon how creative everyone is, which brings me to the #1 rule:

The #1 Rule of the Game

Ok, so I know it seems weird that the most important rule is last... so sue me. But anyways here is the Under-Fire Bible Summerized in two words:

Be creative

That's it, as long as you use your mind, think logically and help out the story, you are making this RPG better for tons of people, me, the players, who ever else decides to join, and most importantly: yourself. Pardon my corny-ness.
