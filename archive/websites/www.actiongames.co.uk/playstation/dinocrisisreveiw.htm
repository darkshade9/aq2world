Dino Crisis
Sunday November 28, 1999

I really really liked the Resident Evil's so I must like Dino Crisis, shouldn't I???

I have many many mixed emotions about this game. First off, it is not what I thought. I thought I would be getting weapons, shooting dinosaurs every 2 seconds, and blowing their heads off (really, I was expecting to be able to blow off dinosaurs heads, which would be cool) But it was not that. Instead I was lucky to encounter a raptor every 5 minutes, again, if I was lucky. This is TOTALLY differant than the way it is handled in Resident Evil 2, where you encounter minimum of 6 zombies per minute.

Graphics

The graphics of Dino Crisis again differ from those in Resident Evils. I am partial to the pre rendered graphics, as they are immensly detailed and very smooth. The backgrounds in Dino Crisis are polygonal, which allows for not much detail at all, but the backgrounds move with you. The backgronds aren't that beutiful compared to those to Resident Evil. Accually, they are quite sterile. Basically the backgrounds look rushed. I do have to say that the dinosaurs are amazing. They are really really well done and Reginas model isn't that bad either. Overall, the Graphics are ok, but I know that Capcom can do alot better, even if they didn't go the pre-rendered route, the backgrounds coul have been better. Overall, I was a bit disappointed.

Gameplay

The gameplay could be summed up in about one sentance: If you can control a resident evil character you can control regina. That is pretty much it! She is exactly handled as the resident evil characters, except for the addition that you can move with your gun aimed. It suprises me, Capcom has tried so hard to make Dino Crisis a new game, but in essence it is the same game with a new story and polygonal backgrounds. Wierd.

Story

I would have to say that this is one of the stronger sides of Dino Crisis. Dino Crisis' story is involving and good. If you want to get into a story, get Final Fantasy 8. If you want story, dinosaurs, and decent graphics, get Dino Crisis.

Sound

As you might already know, I am not a big fan of the sound factor, but some people are, so I might want to mention that the voice acting in this game is decent, but the creepy effects add to the depth, so if you are one that (for some reason) really cares about sound, then you might want to check it out.

Bottom Line

Bottom line is that Dino Crisis looks rushed. If you think about it, it was announced a few months ago and came out about a month ago, it could have been alot better. Also, something I haven't mentioned, is that the puzzles are tough, so if you aren't to smart when it comes to video games, you might want to rent it to see if it is at your level before you buy it.
