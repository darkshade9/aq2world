Member 		posted October 25, 2000 12:02 PM               


*The scene opens upon a quiet little neighborhood somewhere in America in the early morning hours. Some cars pass by to go to work and there are some joggers out getting their exercise. Out of the corner of the camera, a man dressed in black can be seen walking on the sidewalk. He shoulders a few joggers that pass his way. He turns around to the camera and it's Mr. Tokyo. He narrows his eyes at the camera.*

Mr. Tokyo: Everywhere I go you follow me. Can I not get one minute of peace? Do you think I will do something extraordinary or weird? You Americans are obsessed with the invention of the camera.

*Tokyo pulls his hood over his head and continues walking. It starts to sprinkle a little and he tightens his draw string and continues on his way.*

Mr. Tokyo: You know, it is funny what people will say to make themselves feel bigger than what they are. My friend and business partner Scott Hinton says that he is impecable...flawless and perfect in everyway. Crag says that he is an American hero. Marasmus thinks he is important because he has status and seniority over the rest of us. He thinks his titles speak for him.

*Tokyo turns to the rain-spattered camera lens and narrows his eyes.*

Mr. Tokyo: I let nothing nor no one speak for me. I let my actions speak louder than my words. Marasmus, I do not care if you've been half of the Tag Champs. I don't care if you're the .NET champ or the .ORG champ. I don't care about anything like that. I just care about getting you in the ring and bringing some semblance of honor to these titles.

*Tokyo turns back around and continues his trek through the rain.*

Mr. Tokyo: You wanted to be a fighting champion? You wanted to be someone whom those fans adored and admonished? I am not here to bring joy the hearts of the fans. I am here to better my place in the scheme of things. When the wrestling world comes to an end, I want to go down as the greatest. I want to out on top. If you want a fight, then you my friend, have a fight. On Surge, you just sign your death certificate and I'll be happy to take your .ORG from you and give it a name that I can be proud of.

*The scene fades to black as Tokyo continues to walk through the rain to God-knows where.*
