a. Open cement_02-JE[1].jpg

Yep it's cement. A kinda worked flat thing not a lot of character by itself.

NOTE: Now before you do anything else. . .Save As. This way you don't accidentally overwrite the base image. I called mine wall_1

 

b. Crop it

The images dimensions are 745 x 1000... not exactly game proportions. And since most games like to use texture sizes in powers of 2 (8, 16, 32, 64, etc.) we need to whittle it down to size. With your grid on it should be a snap to select out a 256 x 512 area.

``256 x512?! isn't that kinda big?"
Yes it is. . . it's double size as a matter of fact. If you work large then it's easier to work on the details. When you're ready for final output, just scale down to 50%. I

f you're working Q3, you don't need to scale it down, because as you probably already know, the default in Radiant is .5 txtr scaling, so this txtr will wind up at 128 x 256 from the beginning.

It starts at 256 x 512 and gets scaled to 128 x 256.
