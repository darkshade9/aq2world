Knightmare

Position: Quake2 forum moderator

Bios: Started mapping for Q2 about 5 years ago, when I finally ditched my crappy Cyrix for an overclocked PII. Produced a 4-map unit (not yet released). Became frustrated with the entity limitations in Q2, and then merged the source for the official mission packs and Lazarus. During coding this mod, also became frustrated with limitations in the Q2 engine. Upon release of the Q2 engine source, immediately set about "booming" the engine (Boom was a Doom engine mod that drastically increased that game's mapping limits) to increase the limits and fix bugs that most annoy mappers. Current projects: KMQuake2 engine/GPL Lazarus and Pax Gold Edition.

Mazemaster

Position: Forum Moderator

Bios:I started editing Half-Life maps in 1999, and quickly found my way to the RUST forums. These were really the first internet forums I visited regularly, and as such will always feel like home (aww, how cute). With practice and help from the whole community here, especially Merl and Wolv, I became a proficient mapper. Anyways, at first I was only interested in making 3D walkthrough mazes, of which I made many, cluminating in the preposteriously difficult megamaze, but over time I shifted towards regular level design. I have created many maps for HLSP, HLDM, CS, and various other Half-Life mods. My 3 most popular maps are King of the Castle (KotC HLDM), Megalith2 (HLDM), and de_nightfall (CS), whereas my personal favorites are Catacomb (HLDM), deviant_sn (HLDM), and rc_redrock (HLRally, mod unreleased). I love to tinker with mapping and create new effects or combinations of entities that no one has seen before, such as making a random events generator in Half-Life with env_beams, or creating a motion blur effect by intentionally and strategically creating a HOM (hall of mirrors) "error" in certain places. These days I am getting into modeling, coding, and 2d art, all three of which I am slowly but surely becoming proficient at.

Miniwood

Position: Forum Moderator

Bios: I am ancient. I first got into video games when I got an Atari 2600 for Christmas 1978 (Yes I did mean that ancient). After a couple of years of irritating begging I got my first computer, a Tandy/Radio Shack TRS-80 with a massive 4K of RAM. Being a precocious little sod I quickly learnt the Z80 assembler and made my own version of Space Invaders. However, computer games at this time did not live up to my expectations. The technology did not catch up with my imagination until the release of Wolfenstein 3D (I don't have a great imagination, I dream in 16 colour EGA). Then when Quake and Quake 2 came out, with real freedom of movement and easy to use tools, I got hooked on mapping and found RUST. I've made maps for Quakes I, II & III, Half-Life, Thief & Thief II, Kingpin, No-One Lives Forever, Unreal and Deus Ex. I have only released one of them, a DM map for Quake II, which I considered marginally less crap than the others. I plan to map for Half-Life 2, Doom III, and Quake IV and I'm looking forward to those games immensely. In real life I am a full-time carer. My wife, a psychotherapist, is ill and needs daily help due to life-long asthma and several complications. I am also a part-time, freelance writer and editor. I am a non-smoker with a good sense of humour and my own car.

Links to Work http://Miniwood.com

Neur0

Position: Forum Moderator

Bios: About 5 years ago I started playing Q2, it didnt take long for me to find QuArK and try to make my own maps. For years it was just for a laugh and make silly maps to just play with my friends. Along the way I did learn a thing or two about QuArK, and how the Q2 engine likes its maps best. Ive never made any (finished) DM maps cause Im too much of a detail sucker to come up with something I find good enough to actually release. A couple of years ago I made #QuArK on quakenet and started helping out people with the editor, giving them tips and making small example maps to give them more insight on how to use entities. At about the same time I got involved with the Q2 mod 'Jump' that was coded for and hosted by Jolt. After a while i became admin there. As you all know Jolt has ended their Q2 support, so at that time I found a new host, made a website and transferred the entire Jolt jumping comunity to the new domain. Since Pooy, the original mod coder ended the jump project as far as his coding for it goes, it was me who kind of became the mod runner. So a new team was assembled and today the mod is still doing good with a dcecent server load and player comunity. I was also (rcon) admin for wireplay for a while for Q2 KotS (king of the server) mod, untill they pulled the plug on that one. My goal as far as games, internet and comunity goes, is to try and help keep my little corner of Q2 as alive and active as I can. I may have found a new coder for a biggish project Ive got planned for the JumpMod, so Im hoping this will work out. Its nothing much, but Im just doin' me lil part

Wolv

Position: Forum moderator

Bios: After some tinkering with Doom II maps way back I didn't do much editing for quite a while until I went to university and got internet access. I found this nifty little editor called QuArK and started mapping a little for Quake II and later for Half-Life, the latter being the only game where I ever finished a map for. Right now I mostly hang out at the Natural Selection forum, drooling over the maps but never actually building one.
