Half-Life 2 is being reviewed!
Submitted by Andy on Wednesday, October 6, 2004 - 05:29

Half-Life 2

   If you haven't heard all the stuff going around about Half-Life 2, then you either live in a cave or just don't care. Many of us have been awaiting the sequel since the first version of HL shipped, it was just so damn good.

Gordon Freeman hard at work

   Actually it wasn't just damn good, it was mind blowing. So it comes as no surprise that the initial reviews of Half-Life 2 have hit the streets screaming "Mind blowingly brilliant".

   The original Half-Life launch wasn't smooth, The creators decided to swap engines just before release and left everyone wondering 'WTF". The release of Half-Life 2 seems to follow suit, but in buckets. Not only did some of the game code get lifted from Valve, but there were legal wrangles and other delays.

   Last month, as we reported here at RUST, the Precach of HL2 shipped via Steam to those who had pre-ordered it. As soon as that happened, the news sites went mad predicting "Gold by Monday". The sad reality was that we are still waiting, so it is interesting to see that some very reputable game sites have reviewed the game already, and yet the places I thought I would see news have none.

   How can that be? It seems that some of the Major Gaming publications have been given a sneak peak. To keep our content up to date, here is a summary of what is being said:
  • Bask in the light of Gordon Freeman's geek Messiah status.

  • A symphony of shooting, a Ballard of blastery.

  • Gordon's Story finds a new and astonishing trajectory in a brutal dystopia.

  • Own This
I wasn't going to include that last bit, but it really does sum up everything I have read. So to let you in on a few things that jumped out of the pages at me, here is a summary:

   The first thing that came through loud and clear was that there are no cutscenes. Yep, so they say. I couldn't imagine HL without cutscenes but someone at Valve must have been listening to the players, because when you want to get in there and shoot baddies, the last thing you want to do is watch a cleverly crafted camera sequence... If I want to watch a movie, I will go to one.

   Half-life 2 is played out through Gordon's eyes, you embrace him and become him as he arrives in City 17 (bet they reserved the webaddress?) which represents a near future reality. Characters abound and interaction is the only way to get anywhere. Alyx, Eli, Barney and Dr Kleiner are your partners in this rollercoaster adventure, but the reviews were quick to point out that they are not your only allies in this game. They do however setup the initial cooperative game play issues that will see you through the mind boggling landscapes and firefights.

   Apparently the game unfolds in an almost intuitive way. This is something that Valve have always had success in achieving. They always managed to keep it fun and exciting. But the thing that caught my eye was the use of a central tower. This was the greatest thing about Half-life. The ability to see where you needed to go and sometimes what was in store for you. This type of 'loop' mapping is difficult to achieve, but if executed well, the rewards are endless.

   One review quoted the game as a breath taking visual menagerie, and I don't doubt it having seen the screenshots. It is the little things that make a dull level game so much more enjoyable, like rubbish in a stairwell or a broken pipe. Valve has apparently provided such a realistic environment that it is sometimes hard to imagine you not actually there.

   The Big Key to Half-Life 2 is the interaction between Gordon and physical objects. Finally you will get to pick things up, throw them, smash them and generally play havoc with your game environment. A Gravity Gun that allows you to pick up solid objects and hurl them away at will also aid you. At last we get to interact with everything.

   It wouldn't be Half-Life without some special weapons and modes of transport, and there are these in spades. Attention to detail, a story that unfolds and a playability beyond belief is what we can expect. I initially had a dreadful feeling that the whole thing was going to be a let down. But hey my glass is half empty and I like it that way. So far I haven't seen anything to tickle my pessimistic fancy. All I have seen is pure brilliance.

   The more I read the more I realised that those who had been lucky enough to play the game were not designers. Players sure, but they were not looking at it through the eye's of a creator.

   I guess that you and I will have to wait untill it is out before we can get that insight.
