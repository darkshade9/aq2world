Nvidia announces the NV40 Chipset
Submitted by Peeves on Thursday, April 15, 2004 - 02:10

April 13, 2004 NVIDIA hosted a LAN party in California USA revealing its new graphics card - Geforce 6800. Utilizing the new NV40 Chipset the Geforce 6800 delivers an amazing array of new features dominating every consumer based graphic card on the market today. The Geforce 6800 has amazing features such as support for Direct X 9's Pixel shader 3.0, 16 textures per rendering pass, 16x anisotropic filtering (with the Intellisample 3.0 technology) and more. For those of you who like games that use volumetric shadows (a.k.a. Stencil Shading) this is a must card for you to own, the new NV40 chipset has increased performance focusing on the shadow technologies used in games such as Doom 3, Far Cry and other games that utilize the volumetric shading technology.


Things to definitely focus on this new graphics card are its amazing hardware features.
- Over 220 Million Transistors
- Designed to run like butter on 8x AGP Slots
- 16x Texture Pipeline (it actually has 8 however emulates and runs at 16)

The competition over the next months will be growing as ATI steps in and tries to beat NVIDIA, however unlike previously, both NVIDIA and ATI pumped up their security to make sure the cards are as discreet as possible. Not much is known about what ATI is working on, what is known is that it has 12x Texture Pipelines (current ATI cards have 8). We will only know within the next couple of months who comes out the leader. However it is most likely going to be a very close power race just like months ago with the geforce fx and the ATI 9600+ series.

You can find out more information about the NV40 Chipset, see amazing videos demonstrating its power and view specifics on the Geforce 6800 at www.nvidia.com.
