BIOGRAPHY

- DESCRIPTION:
Lets see... I think the first game I ever played was some sort of face arrangment game on an Atari. I was waaaay too little to be sure, though. First games I really got into were the classics: Doom, Wolfenstien 3D, Quake (to play games I had to crack my dad's password on his funky DOS shell). I started getting interested in mapping when my dad's friend showed me a doom2 map he was working on. I promptly filled up 3 graph paper pads with maps. Later, when I actually got the change to use the Doom 2 editor (don't ask me which one, I cant remember), I quickly though "wow, what a piece of shit" and stopped using it. Then Came Unreal. Unreal came bundled with a map editor. And it was a piece of shit. But not in options wise, just stability. So, after learning all the "do not do this or it will crash" features of UnrealED, I spent many a long day mapping away. Then I came across an old copy of quake and decided to give mapping Quake a spin, but the whole "constructive" geometry was above my Unreal-indoctrinated mind. Somewhere around here I was all excited about the new games coming up, primarily Prey. Looked like an awesome engine. There was also this "Half-Life" game coming out too, but I didn't think it would be as cool as Prey. Yeah, go me. First time I loaded up Half-Life I was hooked for the next week solid. I could not pry myself away. As soon as I beat it, it was time to look for how to map for it. Which brought me right back to Worldcraft. "Ok, this time I will find help". The help being, of course, Rust's forums, where I proceeded to bug Merlin's Cat constantly for help. Eventually, the things Merl imparted to me I started to impart to others, and my love of helping others with mapping was born. Lets fast forward a bit here, as we fly through the next few years. Its now a lot later, and I'm still playing Half-Life. I pretty much have the entire mapping side of Half-Life figured out by now, but I'm a fickle mapper, and so nothing really leaves my HDD. I start writing tutorials so that I can just post a link instead of answer the same damn forum question a million times. Fast forward a bit more, and I see that Rust is back, and start porting articles. After the Rust staff shows themselves to be more.... involved then the VERC staff, I switch to Rust as my primary work. After much bitching about things, the Rust staff decides to include me, based partly on some misconception that I am "old school". :P And here we are, back in the present.

- MAPPING INTERESTS:
Absolutely anything having to do with Half-Life. I've done Unreal before, but I definitely favor the CSG.

- SKILLS:
I am profiecent with Hammer/Worldcraft and photoshop. I also have just about every internet language you can think of down, except Perl (my java is a little bit iffy, but thats because I loathe java).

- OTHER:
Rest of my life revolves around my son, my wife, my guns, and my photography.

 

PORTFOLIO

- WORK:
Work? I wish.

- MOD's AND LEVEL'S:
PQL - A really fruity map pack made by a bunch of VERC regulars. The idea was that each person makes a map without knowing what the other maps are, then we put them all together. Each level change brings you to a new place, hence the Quantum Leap part of the title. My map is verc_27, if you want to look. Not much, pretty much a hallway and a couple rooms. Nothing particularly bad, nor particularly special.

- MODELS:

All of my custom textures are currently sitting in my mod folder waiting for use. After real life kicks me in the nuts again and I realise I dont have the time to make a mod, I'll probably release them.
