Unreal Tournament 2004: Editor's Choice Edition Planned For Fall

Submitted by RaVenous on Mon, 2004-08-16 23:30.
After receiving many awards from various magazine and online publications, Epic announced that they will be re-releasing Unreal Tournament 2004 as Unreal Tournament 2004: Editor's Choice Edition. The gameplay will remain the same as the original game. But, what is different in the re-release is the number of added extras. The biggest addition to the game will be the three new vehicles available in Onslaught mode, the Cicada, the Paladin, and the Self-Propelled Mobile Artillery (or SPMA). The Cicada is two-man flying vehicle where one person can pilot while the other mans a belly turret. The new Paladin vehicle is a heavy tank that has both offensive and defensive capabilities. As for the SPMA, you can get an idea of what it is just by reading its name.

Citizen-132 is Recruiting

Submitted by oirfid on Mon, 2004-08-09 16:45.

The FarCry MOD Citizen-132 is recruiting for the following positions on their team:

Sandbox Mapping : People with experience in FarCry level design. If you are interested, send us some examples of your work. Texturing and Static Modeling are not required, but if you can do that, send us some pictures of that as well.

Animation : People who can rig and animate characters and weapons. If you think you have the skills (don't be afraid even if you doubt your own talent, we look at every application), send us a demo reel or something similar.

Skinners/Texturers : If you can skin or texture characters or static models, show us some work. You won't be required to model either if you don't want, but we need some decent people. Show us some work of yours, either 2d or 3d.

Character Modelers : People who can model, as we could really use 2 more character modelers. Citizen-132 has many demented creatures that need to be done, so we need the extra help.

Citizen-132 is a Action/Horror mod using the FarCry engine. Read more about this project in our forums or at the Citizen-132 web site.

RUST REBORN: Forum Merger Completed

Submitted by Gard on Mon, 2004-08-09 03:47.

On the 20th of September 2001 disaster struck the game mapping community when the RUST Forums of the day were hacked. In the last few days of the old WWWThreads Rust Forum some idiot was constantly posting as other people. The_Relentless was less than impressed when the hacker posted as him in this thread. Before long the hacker just went crazy with numerous stupid posts. Finally the Administrators of the day removed the old forums and a few days later created new forums, which are still very much in use today. Unfortunately at the time it was deemed too complicated to merge the old forums with the new forums, as the SQL’s were just so different. Thus the hacker won, and RUST lost many of its best contributors. Many of whom moved on or just left the game making community.

That was the worst day in RUST’s history. Now a new team of RUST Administrators and Moderators have rebuilt RUST. Have resolved the SQL problems and brought back the old lost RUST Forum’s and merged them with the new Rust Forums. Thus completing RUST.

At Gamedesign.Net we never forget anyone who contributes to RUST. Every post that sheds light into the art of MOD making is valuable. Every post, tutorial, news story is part of our history and will be preserved. We are not just any game making map community we are the BEST!

It is with great pleasure that I inform you RUST is now whole again, and like the Phoenix from the fire we will get stronger. We never forget those that contribute. Those that inspire us to be the best mappers, modelers, game designers. So In tribute to everyone who is contributing or has contributed to RUST’s history, we have decided to post the names of the all time best RUST contributors (in order of total posts). If you know anyone on this list … tell them RUST’s says thanks!

Merl (Merllins Cat)
