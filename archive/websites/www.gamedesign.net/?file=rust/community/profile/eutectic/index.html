Eutectic

    Community Profile

    Eutectic has been around the editing community for as long as I can remember, sending me corrections and more info on too many tutorials to mention. And who can forget all those awesome prefabs he did. Some of the most amazing stuff I've ever seen!

Rust: For those out there that don't know, tell us a little about yourself.

Eutectic: WHO am I? Good question! ...

Hmm... I'm not quite sure these days... Mother!!... Mother, leave that shovel alone!... Sorry... What were you saying?... Oh yes! ... Well I can tell you WHAT I am. I'm basically what you could define as a failed engineer/overqualified technician. The main problem with this is that the title doesn't fit in standard job application forms :)


Rust: How long have you been into gaming in general?

Eutectic: Since the day I got my first PC (a 486-33Mhz) back in the days of Wolfenstein "3D". So not that long compared to the *real* hardcore gamers.


Rust: What are your favorite games of all time?

Eutectic: Hmmm... That would have to be all the FPS game that include modern weaponry like machine guns, shotguns, explosives, etc. Not very original perhaps but like I said, I don't consider myself as an "avid gamer".

Strategy games, simulations, magic spells, sorcerers and stuff like that don't do much for me. In fact, if FPS games didn't exist, I wouldn't be into games at all. Oops! Did I just give JC's ego another boost here? ;)

Rust: Do you have any maps released or currently working on any?

Eutectic: I hate to say this but ... no, I never completed a map. And I'm not working on one these days because I'm mainly concentrating on keeping the SiN editing community "alive and kicking" with tutorials, answering questions, documenting stuff, etc. I also still have a lot of stuff to figure out about Sin editing. This occupies ALL of my spare time. I definitely want to make a decent SiN map but that won't be before a while.


Rust: What are your thoughts on Sin editing compared to Q2 editing, and why haven't as many people moved over to it?

Eutectic: Simple. SiN editing beats the living daylight out of editing maps for Quake2 or any other 3D game because it's at least a 1000 times more flexible and powerful. The downside to this is the extra complexity involved. There are several reasons why less people than expected moved over to it IMO.

The first is that a big part of the experienced map designers which had the necessary knowledge base to learn Sin editing quickly now work for game companies. So for newcomers, the learning curve is kinda steep.

The second reason is mainly subjective: as everybody knows, the game itself disappointed a lot of people because of the original bugs and long loading times. This was compounded by the (un)timely arrival of Half-Life which contrasted heavily with SiN in all those aspects (especially the loading times) plus the fact that it knocked everybody's socks off as a game. So a large number of "potential" mappers "fell in love" with Half-Life and found the idea of making maps for that game more compelling than editing maps for Sin. For my part, I had wrongly assumed that most map designers would, like me, fall in love with the power of SiN editing instead.

But it ain't over yet. I'm hoping that my efforts and SinCTF can help to "turn the tables".


Rust: Any future games coming out soon that you plan on editing or making a level for?

Eutectic: I'll certainly take a look at Quake3 Arena of course. Gotta keep up with the latest technologies after all. But there's so much work left to do and so many things to explore with SiN map editing that it will keep me busy for a good while still. The Q3A engine will undoubtedly introduce some interesting technological novelties but I think it's worth mentionning that there's a lot of editing features in SiN that even Q3A won't have: a scripting language, scriptobjects, scalable game models, etc.

In many aspects, SiN is years ahead of its time in terms of editing technology. So the only game I'm really planning on making levels for at this time is SiN because there is basically no limit to what you can create with it.


Rust: You made some of the coolest prefabs ever seen for Quake 2. How long did it take you to make some of those prefabs, and which is your favorite?

Eutectic: Thank you. Some only took a couple of hours but others have taken up to 3 days to complete.

The one I was most complimented about was by far my second turret but the one I enjoyed making the most is a kind of a blowing fuse switch. The challenge lied in the fact that the thing emits pulsing light and the light had to fade out no matter when the fuse was shot during the pulsing cycle. Easier said than done with only hardcoded entities and no scripting language.


Rust: Do you plan on making any more prefabs?

Eutectic: Maybe but those would be intended more as example maps or scripts rather than actual prefabricated things to be popped in a designer's map. Sort of like "tutorial" or "how to" prefabs if you will.


Rust: Seen any good movies lately?

Eutectic: My attorney has advised me not to answer that question.


Rust: What do you do in your free time besides mapping?

Eutectic: Free time? What free time? :)


Rust: Any closing thoughts, comments, rants?

Eutectic: A riddle:

    The very moment you call my name, I cease to exist. Who am I?
Whoever can solve it wins ... a cookie ;)
