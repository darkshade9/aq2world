Geoffrey DeWan

    Community Profile

    Out of the handful of people that really understand the Quake 2 engine and compilers, Geoffrey DeWan has been one of the most helpful people on the Quake 2 discussion board. He has modified versions of all 3 Quake 2 compilers and he actively keeps them updated which allows them to run faster and include more features than the default Id compilers.

Rust: For those out there that don't know, tell us a little about yourself.

Geoffrey DeWan: I am a 33 year old computer programmer for a company that makes flight training devices. These are airplane (with a few heli's scattered in there for good measure) used to primarily train pilot how to fly using instruments. In my spare time I like to read science fiction and play games (actually spend more time playing table top games than computer games).


Rust: What inspired you modify the compilers? Were you looking to just make them faster? Or, were you interested in adding new features of fixing bugs?

Geoffrey DeWan: I originally modified qbsp3 and qrad3 to be able to read texture information right out of a pak file when I heard BSP was going to have that ability, and originally, I was just going to keep these for my own personal use. But eventually I decided to release them. At this point, things kind of took a life of its own. As for the speed, that was simply accomplished by running them through a better optimizing compiler.


Rust: What inspired you to write the tutorials you've done?

Geoffrey DeWan: Only have written one (about hint brushes) so far. Mainly wrote that because I felt there was a lot of misinformation about how to use it. Hopefully I cleared things up a bit. It can be found at: http://users.net66.com/~gdewan/hintdesc.html


Rust: After spending as much time as you have on the discussion boards, what do you think is the largest problem that beginner level designers struggle with?

Geoffrey DeWan: Hard to say. I don't actually read all the threads so I don't really get a feel for what most people have problems with.


Rust: What games are you really looking forward to in the future?

Geoffrey DeWan: Right now the one I am most looking forward to is the Babylon 5 Combat Simulator. Don't know what is going on with it though as Sierra laid off the original team working on it, but I still have hopes. Oh, and in the pipe dream category, a remake of M.U.L.E. One of the all time great games.


Rust: How long have you been programming?

Geoffrey DeWan: Since..1982? I think it was 1982.


Rust: What are your thoughts on the future of the user made level editing community?

Geoffrey DeWan: Not sure. With all these editable games out, I think it is getting diluted. However, as more and more people get connected, there is a lot more people to dilute. It is going to turn from a small town to New York City. A lot more people, but people with interact with a smaller number of people.


Rust: Seen any good movies lately?

Geoffrey DeWan: I'm really looking forward to The Phantom Menace. Can't really say what the last movie I really enjoyed was but I must confess to being slightly entertained by The Corrupter and Payback.
