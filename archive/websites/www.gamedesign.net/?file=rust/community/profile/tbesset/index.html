Timothee Besset

    Community Profile

    As most of you already know, TTimo (his IRC nick) is the man behind the updates to Q3Radiant. He is also responsible for bringing the Loki Games pet-project of a cross-platform version of GtkRadiant to the masses.

Rust: Lets start with the obligatory, "who is TTimo?"

Timothee Besset: I guess that's me. French guy, hooked to Id games since Q1, been mostly involved with the level editing community.


Rust: Currently you the man on the development of Radiant, the Q3 map editor. How did you land in that boat?

Timothee Besset: When Q2 came out and 3D boards started being in every computer, I realized the Q2 engine allowed you to do a lot more than what you could reasonably do with Radiant. I didn't want to start writing an editor from scratch so I started working on MGS (http://mgs.qeradiant.com). That's how I got in touch with Robert Duffy, and started doing more and more stuff around level editing. I ended up being contracted shortly by Id (summer 99), and when Robert Duffy got hired by Id and worked on Quake III: Team Arena I kinda became the maintainer of the public version of the editor.


Rust: What was your first contribution to the gaming community?

Timothee Besset: It was MGS. The first big thing for me was having some of MGS code making it's way into QERadiant (the Q2 editor) for texture locking when rotating brushes.


Rust: Where would you like to see yourself in 2 years?

Timothee Besset: Working for a game company (as owner? heh, who knows). What could be better than getting money out of what you like to do?


Rust: How long have you been programming and what was your first programming project? In retrospect, how successful was it?

Timothee Besset: My first programming project was "PC Boy". It was designed for CGA/EGA computers, like my old Amstrad PC 1512. Written in basic .. I started to learn from there, I was 12.


Rust: What is your favorite game of all time?

Timothee Besset: I discovered computer games with Mario Bros. Spent endless hours playing it. Quake 1 was the next big thing, a real fascination.


Rust: What was the last movie you saw and what did you think?

Timothee Besset: X-Men, watched it on video with my nephews. Cool stuff.


Rust: Last thing: Quick word association... (think fast!) -- I'll give you a word or so and you whip out a quick and dirty answer.

Timothee Besset:

    id software - games, and then more. Best damn artists I've ever seen.
    gtk - radiant (sorry)
    radiant - 1.2 .. someday .. if god is with us
    new doom - wish I could be a part of that
    3am - it's in 1 hour
    car - I'm getting rid of it soon, damn I hate cars
    female - damn she has been supporting my geek life for 5 years now, I must be very lucky.
    television - so boring and full of shit. but I can't turn it off
