AlienvsPredator. Iam working on a level with the inhouse importers/exporters. Love to work in max, dont do any levels if they cant give me the right tools. I have found out about many render engines. Quake is stable and easy to locate errors, but you cant create good outdoor levels cause the use of solids/face limitations. Fusion (Descent3) and this Avp engine swallow every mesh. In Avp every room is a single mesh, and are refered as modules. That meens its mutch faster to create levels, you can use face extrude etc. There are many good renderengines out there. Maybe you wanna try out a new one? -I dont like to use a shitty ED just beacuse they dont want to realise the tools they used. If there is always two-set of tools, one for public and one for inhouse. Then we cant ever educate our self as game designers. And that stinks. Just Rendered my perspective viewport. I like the renderquallity from 3dsMax. Everything works great within max, but if something within the game is wrong, the screen is flickering, and its hard to know why... johan Sunnebjer alias djmj http://hem.passagen.se/studio14/

Magrittes Recamier
[ 2001-11-02 | sent in by Bryn Mosher ]

My hardest map model yet is done. This one takes some explaining. First, Jacques-Louis David did a painting called Madame Recamier (on the bottom left). Rene Magritte then did a more morbid version replacing the lovely lady with a sitting coffin (on the bottom right). After that Magritte made a bronze sculpture from his painting (bottom middle). Lastly, good old BadMonkey came along and put the sculpture/painting into Quake 3. This model may look simple, but it weighs in at 1,154 polygons! www.networkoftheapes.net
