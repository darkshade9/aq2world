Who is Schroet Kommando?
Submitted by G.A.R.D. on Monday, February 16, 2004 - 01:59

It is the dawn of a brave new world, where computer geeks of all shapes and sizes can and are becoming the next generation of world champions. Earning over $100,000 and signing sponsorship deals with the big companies, and they are doing all of this without leaving their computer screens. These are the new super athletes, with supreme physical dexterity, adapted to hyper-auto-responses from changes in photoelectric plasma screens. Athletes who are intellectually gifted with high spatial reasoning and cognitive processing skills. Put simply, people who excel at playing computer games, such as FPS (“First Person Shooters”). These competitors / combatants meet on fields that don’t exist, whilst at the same time they can be on opposite sides of the Earth. This new world I speak of is the virtual world, where the computer users of this world can become legends, win money, get sponsorship deals and experience the rivalary of being the worlds best.


2002 Picture of a CPL Gaming Convention (source CNN)


Whilst all that may seem far fetched, recently, Cyberathelete Professional League (CPL), announced a $50,000US Call of Duty (CoD) tournament. Which will take place July 28 to August 1, 2004. At the brand new Gaylord Texan Resort & Convention Center in Grapevine, Texas. The Cyberathlete® Extreme World Championships, sponsored by the Intel® Pentium® 4 processor with HT Technology Extreme Edition. A $50,000US prize just for being the best at a computer game is just the start. More and more computer gaming competitions are happening every day. It won’t be long until before the Professional Computer Gamer becomes a reality.

So Who is Schroet Kommando? Schroet Kommando isn’t just one person, but a team of six multi-national, semi-professional computer athletes, who are widely considered to be the best "Counter-Strike" team in the world, and are currently the raining CPL World Champions. The team consists of a six-man multi-national squad. To date Schroet Kommando total winnings is around $107,165US. But their net worth could be much higher from website advertising, promotional work and all the perks of being the best in their game. Recently Schroet Kommando signed a sponsorship agreement to train and compete on PCs equipped with NVIDIA GeForce FX graphics processing units (GPUs), as well as playing a role in the company's grassroots marketing campaigns geared towards gaming enthusiasts worldwide. All this from just playing computer games. I remember when I used to get in trouble for staying up late to play computer games. Now I can envisage some parents saying to their children; “Keep practicing, else you will never be good enough to make Schroet Kommando”

This year CPL Is running the following four tournaments:

- $100,000 Counter-Strike Tournament
The sponsor of this tournament is the Intel® Pentium® 4 Processor with HT Technology Extreme Edition

- $50,000 Call of Duty Tournament
The sponsor of this tournament is the Intel® Pentium® 4 Processor with HT Technology Extreme Edition.

- $50,000 Battlefield 1942
The sponsor of this tournament is the Intel® Pentium® 4 Processor with HT Technology Extreme Edition.

- $50,000 Halo PC Tournament
The primary sponsor of this tournament is NVIDIA.

Is this a real trend? Will we one day see computer gamer events on the news like we see golf, tennis, soccer … etc? The answer is yes, as the prize money keeps going up, as there are more online places to bet on these events such as ESReality.com ESReality.com it won’t be long that clans like Schroet Kommando are house hold names.

What does this mean for Gamedesigners, Mappers ... the kind of people who you find on this web site? The guys who design, construct the worlds that computer games inhabit. Well it means more people will play more levels, ask for better quality maps, will seek out the best level makers. It means more opportunity and better mapping days ahead!
