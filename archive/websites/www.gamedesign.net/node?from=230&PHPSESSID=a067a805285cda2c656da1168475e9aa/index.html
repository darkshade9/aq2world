Human Head Studios Hiring

Submitted by Method on Mon, 2004-06-07 19:06.
Human Head Studios, Incorporated is looking for an animator to add to their collection of heads. This is a full time, on-site position with benefits for a project using the DOOM 3 Engine.

In order to be considered for this job, they require that you be able to demonstrate the following:

1. Thorough understanding of animation fundamentals
including posing, timing, and character weight.
2. Be able to rig character models for animation
3. Working technical knowledge of Maya Animator.
4. Willing to accept direction and work well under pressure.
5. Self motivated and a desire to work with a team.

Quake 4 won't be shown on QuakeCon this year

Submitted by Method on Sun, 2004-06-06 03:11.
PlanetQuake got news about QuakeCon 2004. Todd Hollenshead (Id Software CEO) said that they won't be showing Quake 4, which is being developed by Raven Software with Id Software's supervision, on QuakeCon this year. They're happy with what they've done so far, but they want to make sure it's perfect before showing it to the public. Read full article here.

Interplay offices closed by state officials

Submitted by Method on Sun, 2004-06-06 03:00.
Gamespot got current news on Interplay. The Orange County Register reports that California labor investigators have shut down the publisher. "The company has not shut down. [The state] can’t do that. It can only let me not let employees work," Herve Caen said. Read full article here.

DOOM Movie

Submitted by Method on Fri, 2004-06-04 19:02.
Todd Hollenshead (Id Software CEO) revealed that Warner Brothers, who had the right to the DOOM movie, passed on the option of making it. DOOM's movie license has been picked up by Universal Pictures now. The movie's script is based on DOOM 3, which is as stated by Dark Horizons will be shipped on July 15th.

Interplay out of business?

Submitted by Method on Fri, 2004-06-04 18:09.
After Interplay's financial problems, there was a few rumors that their office is closing and they're going out of business. Yesterday, GameSpot talked with the surprisingly affable Caen, who denied the end was nigh for Interplay. His proof? "You can see our stock is trading, so we're still here," said Caen. So there's still a hope for Kingpin and Fallout fans to see a sequel to their favorite games. Read full article on GameSpot.
