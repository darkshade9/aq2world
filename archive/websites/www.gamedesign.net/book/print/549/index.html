The Online Mappers Resume Database - Developed by RUST

Rust Resume's is a prototype database, for listing and keeping track of the latest gaming work by Mappers, Models, Texture Artists and Coders. The database is designed to show the best of your work, provide links to all your material and help people find you.

To add yourself to the RUST Resume's Merely Fill in the following, provide any images of your work you would like to display, and Register to RUST (This merely ensure's your email address is current and you are who you say you are).

To post your Gaming Resume here click the add comment below, and you will be added in the next few days. Post any additional material, such as pictures to Oirfid or Gard

Every six months RUST will email to confirm the contact details, and work profile is upto date.

    NAME: Andy Howard
    ALIAS: Andy, Methuselah, BinGo'nFishin, Mandy.

    BIOGRAPHY

    - Description:
    I am old by most game design standards which has it's advantages and disadvantages.

    Prior to HL I was involved in a project to convert DooM monster sprites into 'Local Identities' to give us something to do on nightshift. I started using WordCraft 2.1 in '99 or so when I discovered it on the HL CD. First major triumph was a room with a prefab metal shelf that I could bang a crowbar on, how cool is that!! I was hooked.

    I will be the first to admit that my work isn't all that brilliant and this is where the transition began. I moved into adding content to TWHL in 2002.

    Answering questions and keeping things moving by providing tutorials on demand. I employed the test, re-test theory to all the questions I answered and was inspired by FireBinder's Test Chamber. The more time I spent researching questions, the less time I spent on serious HL mapping.

    There were brief interludes to create a few DoD 3.1 maps and a CS map. The CS map was created to see what all the fuss was about, I mean, after all... how hard could it be?

    Bloody difficult was the reality. I ended up reading all of Dave J's design notes and finally rebuilt the whole map.

    The serious mapping pretty much ended there, I realised that I didn't have enough talent to produce quality map's and I decided to utilise my talents guiding and coaching those that had more talent, but didn't understand how to get their dreams to come alive.
