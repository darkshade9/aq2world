Building a Sin Map

Well give yourself a pat on the back if you've made it this far. Ok.. you didn't really pat yourself on the back did you!?!

Seriously though, this tutorial will help you to build a basic room in a Sin map. I don't want to get too complex here because you should use your imagination to expand upon what I'm showing you in this tutorial.

Anyways, lets get started...



Ok first I've gone ahead and contructed the walls and floors of our room. To draw a brush you simply just Left Click in the XY View and drag the mouse to form a brush. Then you can move it around and ajust the height in the Z View. It's probably easier to build the first layer of the walls then select all them and hit Space. Then align the brushes and raise them up so you get the 2 walls on top of eachother effect. Then select the bottom row and apply the texture to them by clicking on a texture in the Texture Picker. Do the same thing for the top row, but with a different texture. After that you can draw in a ceiling brush, and move it in the Z view so that it starts at the top of the walls and is about 8-16 units thick. Then you can copy the ceiling by pressing Space and move it down to make it the floor. Then choose the textures you want for the floor and ceiling.

Notice the brushes I have purposely left selected. This is done so that you can see how multiple textures can be used on a wall or floor, which is used a lot to add detail and depth to today's FPS levels.

Note: All of these textures used can be found in the Dam, Generic/Lights, and Warehouse folders.




Ok now in this phase I've gone and added in a door, that way it looks like you came in the room from somewhere. To add a door all you have to do is draw the brush where you want it and carve it. Yes I said "carve"! Only carve simple shapes and be prepaired to do a little clean up :) Then I recessed the door into the wall 4 units.

The same thing was done with the elevator, except I made two doors. A quick way to flip the texture is to hit the Tx Flip button.

Next I added in some supports in the corners, which was done by making brushes just like I did with the walls and clipping the corners off. To clip you hit Shift+Right Click in the XY View. You will need to put two points and it'll show in the 3D View what will be left after you clip. To perform the clip press enter.



Now I've gone ahead and added in a sign on the wall to let the player know where he is. First make the brush and get it where you want it, then you can use the Fit Texture to Face (in Misc Menu) to line it up.

Then I added in the lights and crates in the same way. You may also want to put in a rotated crate, or a crate on it's side, but I chose not to. In the case that you do, Fit Texture to Face will really help you out so you don't have to bother with rotating the texture yourself.



Ok, now in this last step I've gone and put in 2 characters, who would probably be talking when you enter this room. Maybe even have one of them run for an alarm... it's up to you.

To place the characters or any entity in, you just Right Click in the grid and select what entity to create from the menu that pops up. If you wanted to make the elevator doors work, you would select one of them and Right Click in the grid on the door and select func_door.

Oh yeah, look by the first box and you'll see one of those crazy rats too :)


Conclusions

This was just a really simple map that I put together in about 3 minutes. Imagine what you will be able to do once you get used to SinED!

Remember, watch those damn r_speeds... over 600 is a SiN!!!

Looks like this is the end, you probably know enough to kick out a pretty cool level by now. So grab your favorite CD, check out your favorite editing site for tutorials, and start mapping.

I really hope this helped out a lot of people having trouble with SinED. I know when I first started using QE4 when Quake2 came out I was overwhelmed... but now I look back and can't imagine mapping with anything else. SinED is one powerful editor if you know how to use it :)

If you want you can download the .map file from this tutorial here.
