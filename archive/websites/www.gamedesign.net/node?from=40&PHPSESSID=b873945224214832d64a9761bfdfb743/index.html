A new means to profit from game content?

Submitted by skinmaster on Tue, 2004-11-16 23:25.

A relatively new company called Massive Incorporated is changing the way advertisers deal with the gaming industry. Only a few years ago large companies like Chrysler, Jeep, Pepsi, and Coke delegated very little to no budget for investment in video game advertising. However since these days most kids and "target audiences" spend more time playing games than watching TV, large corporations have begun to spend more money and more time trying to gain profit through advertising in video games. Last year U.S consumers spent more money on gaming paraphernalia than they did at the box office.

Ryan Feltrin Interview

Submitted by Method on Fri, 2004-11-12 21:00.

Ryan Feltrin's Interview

by Method

Please tell us about yourself and how you got into the gaming industry.

Born and bred in Adelaide, Australia, I studied at Blackfriars Priory School, moving onto the University of South Australia, for a Bachelor of Information Science. Never a keen student, but always a keen gamer, I soon found myself getting poor grades, but mastering the art of gaming, and writing useless programs to feed my appetite for creation. After receiving runner-up in my final year project, I got a job working with a lecturer and began my seemingly exciting journey into the professional programming world.
