Fix reports and some new changes afoot...

Submitted by Olpainless on Sun, 2005-02-13 16:10.

As you may have noticed during your regular viewing, we've been making some aesthetic and technical changes to the site, fixing bugs and preparing to enable new features.

First of all, persistent logins are now available, so you won't need to log in every time you view the site; a bonus, I'm sure you'll agree.

All the content is now available again, as is the comment system.

However, the big feature being worked on now is a public submission system, which will allow all of you to submit content to Rust, ready for moderation and eventually incorporation into the site. You can already see the beginning of these labors, as you can submit articles to the editing handbooks, or frontpage news, as well as a rough submission guideline page; however, while the system is ironed out, these articles will not be approved just yet unless considered exceptional. Nonetheless, this feature will be available very soon, and we hope that all the Rust readers will make good use of this new feature. We thank you again for your patience, and hope that you look forward to these changes as much as we do.

Power 2 Mod for Kingpin

Submitted by Method on Sun, 2005-02-06 01:42.
MrDamage of KingpinForever.com and his team are working on a Power 2 Mod for Kingpin. "Some of you may remember the Power Mod, a Capture & Hold style mod, that included three capture points in each map, the ability to hire thugs to defend the capture points, different classes and buy menus where you bought all your equipment. Well after some discussions with Captain Death (who was the final coder on the first version of Power), we have decided to resurrect the mod. We are removing all the buy menus, classes and the hired thugs and focusing on the Capture and Hold component of the mod. The updated version of the mod will be known as Power2 Mod.

Bringing Rust back up to speed...

Submitted by Olpainless on Mon, 2005-01-31 19:09.

We understand that there are still some intermittent problems with the content on Rust, that we are working on resolving as soon as possible - this is related to the system upgrade performed during the christmas/new year downtime.

In order to help, we would appreciate it if you report ANY missing content or errors on the site; if you could include:

  • The page where the error occured (for instance, node/761);
  • What you were doing when the error occurred;
  • And the time of the error, if possible.

This information can help us track down any relevant notices in the logfiles, which will hopefully result in faster resolution of the problem.
