RickNeff
posted 01-26-99 08:23 PM CT (US)          
Well, I think most editors coming over from Q2 have learned that the "big box" method of editing probably isn't the best approach. This isn't to say that they don't plan the level out, but you really should build the proper outer walls for each room.

A lot depends on the type of map you're trying to do. Single player maps, which HL excels at making, require a lot of good looking rooms and a clearly defined objective. This typically means that each room pretty much gets built before moving on to another with the designer deciding how it incorporates into the overall objective/theme.

DM maps on the other hand require a lot of planning but from the perspective of ensuring that players start on a fairly equal footing and providing a more "circular" flow of play. This typically means that rooms don't dead end, often with rooms having multiple exits. It also requires a certain level of planning when it comes to weapons and powerups to keep the "big guns" limited, but enough firepower to stay alive.

But, once the planning is done, I think the method of implementing details and making it look good varies for each individual mapper. Some like to completely finish a room before moving on to the next, while others like to build the basic structure, start filling in with details, architecture, texturing and lighting in different stages.

bushboy
posted 01-26-99 10:55 PM CT (US)          
To texture your maps properly, you have to pay attention to the size of the textures themselves.

If a wall texture is 128 high, then your better of making your wall brush 128 high.

Don't get 'finicky' with walls - build them big - 64 wide is a nice value to use.

For floor and ceiling textures which tile, try and keep the visible part of your floor solids divisable by 64, as most floor textures are 64x64 and tile - so for instance, your floor could be 256x256, or 512x512

As for building, well, everyone has thier own method - I'm sort of inbetween RickNeff and Hudson on this one - some areas I'll complete, while others I'll knock together with basic textures and come back and add detail later.
