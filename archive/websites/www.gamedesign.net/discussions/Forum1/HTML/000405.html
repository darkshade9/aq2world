Assuming you could make that many lights, you would need Deep Blue to light your map. No, that might not be fast enough, maybe The Thinking Machine (you know, that computer the FBI uses to crack supercodes simply by brute force?)
The reason is, when you have all those switchable lights mixing together, Qrad has to calculate the light on all those surfaces for every light in every combination. Since the light will mix or not mix, depending on if they are on or off. So if you had 3 mixing lights, lets see:

SlowHandd
posted 02-15-99 01:34 PM CT (US)          
I can't believe everyone missed the obvious solution to this.

Here's how:

Make a larger ball around your rotating ball and set the properties

surface - light
nodraw
contents - mist (all sides)

Hollow the brush, but keep the inner faces of this area light at least 24 units away from the ball.

You may have to chop of the top half of the area light to make it look decent.

This will work!
