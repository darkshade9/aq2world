RickNeff
posted 02-08-99 01:24 PM CT (US)          
Why are Stroggs there? Well, because they want to be there! Again, like the Mission Packs, it's not hard to make up a reason for them being there (although, does anyone really care? )

Keep in mind that it's supposed to be a war with the Stroggs. So, any reason as to why one side would pick a location to attack or maintain. It could be something as simple as a logistics supply point, such as the case of many of the pacific islands in WWII to both the US and Japan. Or, the Stroggs could have attacked a base/area for something specific - power generator, new heavy weapon, planet resources, etc.

Again, it's not really that hard if you use your imagination. Of course, I wouldn't spend too much time with it, unless it's a TC or Misson Pack since most people probably won't think too much about the WHY aspect - they just want to play the level.

Impaler
posted 02-12-99 09:32 PM CT (US)          
I got an idea for a plot:

You wake up from a violent jolt thirty years later after defeating the Makron (you've been shooting back home for thirty years because you shut down the black hole generator, and therefore have to take the long way home) to find that your pod has been found and taken in by the Strogg Deep-Space Strategic Planning Terminal. You wake up, get out of your pod, and start kicking Strogg butt. You whoop on the station, and head back to your pod, only to find it was jettisoned during the mayhem to further prevent the chance of you actually escaping. You decide to backtrack through the station, which is on autnomous defense system (lasers and proximity target_explosions) into one of the Strogg Emergency Absconding Vessel and shoot down to the designated evasion planet. There you find out that you can charge up your pod and travel through a series of mini-blackholes until you reach Earth. You could stick in a Makron because it is a couple decades later and a new one may have been appointed, but that's your discretion. The planet could probably be your Arctic location, but that may be sort of too Star Wars-ish though. This would be like a 4-6 level series with a lot of level hopping back and forth and would probably need some new texturing and new skies, but I would definitely play it if it was ever made. In my opinion this is following the storyline of Q2 quite closely and would make at least some sense. And this would allow for some believability in the gameplay. Just don't make the Deep-Space thingy a complete copy of Q2's Space Station level. You asked for a reason for Strogg and I gave you (a long) one. -Impaler
