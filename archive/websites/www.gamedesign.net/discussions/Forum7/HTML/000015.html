I really don't understand what the problem is with these sorts of contests? Is it a lot of work, well yeah, but then again, there are plenty of people designing levels, textures, etc. for free as a hobby anyway. Granted you lose "ownership" of the level - which really isn't that big of a deal unless you plan on distributing the level in another format anyway.

Personally, from the company's point of view, heck, just like you mentioned, it's an easy way to get levels for a game without hiring people to do it, with still some sort of compensation.

Basically, it simply comes down to if one feels the level of work is worth the potiential reward. In this case, I dunno if I'd simply want something like game engine rights, but then again, there may be someone who does.

Hardly worth complaining about, IMHO.

Mad Dog
posted 01-23-99 06:00 PM CT (US)          
Here's what I think of this crap (and that's what it is, too):

Rather than take a risk on hiring or contracting a fixed number of employees, these companies can con a lot of starry-eyed "wanna be the next LeveLord" people into sweating their asses off into competing with each other to make a winning level.

Worse than losing right to the work (which would happen if these people were employed instead, anyway), there's no real tangible resume developed.

RickNeff, your experience was at a time when map editing was far less widespread. Now editors are being included with the game when you buy it. The competition is a lot greater under these conditions.

This contest and the recent S3 contest both ask for more than just submitting a map -- these contests require you to work like hell specifically to provide the entry, which would be of no use to anyone anywhere else.

Imagine if someone told you they'd give you a lucrative prize if you won a different kind of contest -- you have to spend a week digging ditches. So you and a hundred other saps dig ditches for this slacker, and then 99 of you walk away with absolutely nothing to show for it.

Of course later this bum might decide he likes your ditch as well, so be sure to sit at home by the phone in case fortune smiles upon you after all.

These contests should be ignored on principle... or maybe not. Maybe whenever one is announced everyone should send a scathing e-mail flame as their submission.

MD

Mad Dog: Ok, I guess this isn't merely directed at you, but I know you won't respond with something along the lines of, "Oh yeh..you @#$%$#@#. This sucks!"

To start with, I certainly wouldn't be entering this contest, nor would I expect too many others to.

However, I'm sorry - If someone is able to make a contest out of digging ditches and still get people to enter, well...something along the lines of natural selection weeding out the weak and stupid comes to mind. And, if a company is able to do get people to enter, well again, I'm sorry, I simply don't see what the problem is.

Granted, a lot of people will work their little butts off in any contest - yet most won't walk away with a thing. And in almost ANY contest that creates a tangible product, the creator often walks away with the rights to the end result. IMHO, it's simply the way contests work.

Of course, I think it's a moot point - I seriously doubt too many people are going to enter such a contest - considering there really isn't even a tangible prize. Which helps to prevent such contests that require a lot of work for no real reward.

And that, IMHO, again is the whole natural selection process at work.
