Community Profile



The Community Profile section is a biweekly section that profiles important people in the gamedesign/level design community.

Geoffrey DeWan


Out of the handful of people that really understand the Quake 2 engine and compilers, Geoffrey DeWan has been one of the most helpful people on the Quake 2 discussion board.  He has modified versions of all 3 Quake 2 compilers and he actively keeps them updated which allows them to run faster and include more features than the default Id compilers.  The compilers can be found here at Rust in the File Server section or at:
 http://users.net66.com/~gdewan/quake2.html

Geoffrey has taken the time to answer a few questions for us, that you can read below.

For those out there that don't know, tell us a little about yourself.

I am a 33 year old computer programmer for a company that makes flight training devices. These are airplane (with a few heli's scattered in there for good measure) used to primarily train pilot how to fly using instruments.

In my spare time I like to read science fiction and play games (actually spend more time playing table top games than computer games).



What inspired you modify the compilers? Were you looking to just make them faster? Or, were you interested in adding new features of fixing bugs?

I originally modified qbsp3 and qrad3 to be able to read texture information right out of a pak file when I heard BSP was going to have that ability, and originally, I was just going to keep these for my own personal use. But eventually I decided to release them. At this point, things kind of took a life of its own.

As for the speed, that was simply accomplished by running them through a better optimizing compiler.



What inspired you to write the tutorials you've done?

Only have written one (about hint brushes) so far. Mainly wrote that because I felt there was a lot of misinformation about how to use it. Hopefully I cleared things up a bit.  It can be found at:
http://users.net66.com/~gdewan/hintdesc.html



After spending as much time as you have on the discussion boards, what do you think is the largest problem that beginner level designers struggle with?

Hard to say. I don't actually read all the threads so I don't really get a feel for what most people have problems with.



What games are you really looking forward to in the future?

Right now the one I am most looking forward to is the Babylon 5 Combat Simulator. Don't know what is going on with it though as Sierra laid off the original team working on it, but I still have hopes.

Oh, and in the pipe dream category, a remake of M.U.L.E. One of the all time great games.



How long have you been programming?

Since..1982? I think it was 1982.



What are your thoughts on the future of the user made level editing community?

Not sure. With all these editable games out, I think it is getting diluted. However, as more and more people get connected, there is a lot more people to dilute. It is going to turn from a small town to New York City. A lot more people, but people with interact with a smaller number of people.



Seen any good movies lately?

I'm really looking forward to The Phantom Menace. Can't really say what the last movie I really enjoyed was but I must confess to being slightly entertained by The Corrupter and Payback.



Thanks to Geoffrey for taking the time to answer these questions.
