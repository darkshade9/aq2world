Game Industry is serious business

Submitted by Peeves on Mon, 2005-04-25 13:49.

With the Industry growing so much, it is no wonder big corporate ass holes are starting to come in and make everything suck. The main thing I am noticing going on with the industry is that money is becomming more important than creativity and having fun. I'm seeing multiple companies especially big ones like EA do the whole "professional" outlook. Tucked in shirts etc. is not what the industry is about. Games in the western world are looked as a laid back hell yes attitude. So should making the games, like it used to be. I have so much respect for Mod teams where a group of kids are working on a mod in their spare time, making any ideas they want into reality and being true to themselves and their goals for their Mod. I don't respect Mod teams that are going the "professional" route. Don't get me wrong, i am a professional and I know how to work on a game properly and all that good stuff, but there is a line between this job totally rocks and this job fucking blows. My job rocks, at NetDevil we are really laid back and have a good time all day and STILL get our work done on time. Many companies are starting to create "sweat shop like environments" as many game developers call them. I can understand it from the "efficency" point of view, but I urge all publishers not to turn us into a cubicle type assembly line. We are artists, designers and thinkers. We are not drones that go home and plug back into the wall only to do the same routine the next day. We come to work wanting to express our freedom of thought in the work we do. I don't want that ruined by corporate cock smack.
