DoD: Source

Submitted by Andy on Wed, 2005-07-27 02:58.

Tim 'Waldo' Holt has released news recently. He expects DoD: Source to ship by the 1st of September.
Many FPS games have tried to emulate DoD, not many still have a following, so it is welcome news indeed. Hopefully it will keep the DoD mapping community alive through the Source conversions.

Speaking of mapping, I finally published a map that is a current favorite on a big Australian custom map server.
Warwarrior helped me bundle and package the final product and it seems to be getting good reviews. The map is small, tight and furious. In a average 15 min game, the map is only capped out once by each team. Young players find it frustrating... Old blokes like me love it.
Patience little grasshopper..

Submitted by Andy on Thu, 2005-03-31 18:07.

Level Design at it's worst, resurecting a former project is not the best idea especially when it was a bad one to start with.
Either way it doesn't matter, the point is that I am re-learning most of what I had convieniently forgotton.

In another forum, someone recently commented about the lack of detail in de_dust. The gist of the discussion was that with a map so poorly built, why was it a great hit?

Answer is simple: neither team had an advantage, any n00b new the layout after one round and it was fun.
