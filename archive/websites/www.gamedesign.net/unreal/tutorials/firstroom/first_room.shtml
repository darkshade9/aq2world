Making your first room

open the editor right click on the cube icon click on load and choose the ancient texture set. choose a good brick texture of some sort


goto cube properties and set the height to 600, width to 1000, and breadth to 900 and click build, then hit close.



Now press the subtract brush from world button
you just subtracted that cube shape from the level to make a room. Remember that unreal editing is quite different then quake2 editing. In Unreal, you carve the shapes from the level. In quake2, you create the individual brushes that will make up the level. It's quite a big difference and takes awhile to get used to it. Now you have your first room! goto the 3d window and move around in it by holding down the left button to move on the xy plane(side to side) and hold both buttons down to move in the xz, yz plane(up and down). Now we are going to change the floor and ceiling textures to something different...click on the floor brush in the 3d window and then click on the texture you want as the floor. very simple. now do the same for the ceiling... now we are going to compile the map. hit f8. and click rebuild geometry

you might of noticed that your 3d view went black...that is because your level is not lit and you are in the dynamic lighting mode. So lets add a light! its a very simple process to add a light. Just decide where you want it...right click and choose add light. now we need to recompile and see the changes...click f8, and rebuild geometry...now you should see your level lighted in the 3d window. The last thing we are missing is a player start. goto the browser tool bar and choose classes. Click on navigation point, and then PlayerStart. Now right click were you want the playerstart to appear and choose add player start here. We are finished! You have just completed your very first unreal map! it sucks..but it's a start. recompile the map....(f8, rebuild geometry) and then save it in the maps/usermade directory. If its not there then make the directory yourself. Now just open windows explorer, find the new map, and double click on it...your done!   look for more indepth tutorials soon, along with a unreal discussion board.
