Japanese Making Half-Life 2 Arcade Game

Submitted by yesukai on Wed, 2005-11-30 04:53.

From Game Brink:

It looks like the Japanese are making a HL2 arcade game. It has a joystick for movement, another joystick for aiming and shooting, then foot pedals for jumping and ducking. Apparently, they get a remixed version of the single player game and multiplayer capability with other arcade machines in Japan. It even has some sort of memory card slot so you can save your game. This is pretty cool sounding. I wonder if we will get any, so I can slam down a roll of quarters for Gordon.

Games in Education: Neverwinter Nights Key Skills

Submitted by Olpainless on Thu, 2005-11-10 04:21.

For the last few months, its been knowledge that I've been working kind-of within games; well, it's time to reveal exactly what I've been working on to the world. My project has been to use a computer game, specifically Neverwinter Nights, to provide an alternative learning environment for Key Skills education.
You can see some of our work at our website, http://www.alteredlearning.com, which will also provide more background to exactly what we've been doing. From the website:
Many learners enjoy playing computer games and so we at Altered Learning decided to take this concept and produce a tailor-made games scenario directly linked to Key Skills qualifications. This move away from paper-based assignments has a dramatic impact on both learner motivation and successes.
