Green, Red AND Blue Entity Lighting.

Ugh, now this has really pushed it too far. Here, we lose one of the saving graces - brightness. No level of brightness is godd enough except 0. However, the placing has again created an interesting effect on the wall. But, let's be honest. The only place you'll find this is in a disco. So, unless you're creating a pastiche on early 80's decor, DON'T DO THIS. It's not realistic, and it sure as hell ain't clever.

Now, to be fair, I have chosen the worst possible case. I've taken the three basic colours, Red, Green and Blue, which are rather far apart in the spectrum. But it does demonstrate a point. Mixing coloured Entity lights can look foul. The problem is, when most people use entity lights, they don't associate them to a source. Now, I have said that you don't have to source light everything (missed that, read back to the Basics section). And you don't. But putting bold colours in like this is really pushing it. I would recommend to anyone putting entity lighting in to at least choose a colour that is close to the rest of the lighting in the room, unless it is for special effect.
