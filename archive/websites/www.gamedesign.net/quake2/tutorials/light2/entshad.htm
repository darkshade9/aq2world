Entity Lit Shadows

This image clearly shows the defined shadows provided by lighting a scene with an entity light. The entity is effectively a point light - much like the sun. Because of this, the light is easily stopped by objects (occluders). The light doesn't seep round the side of the occluder, because the source is smaller than the width of it. Unfortunately, this can sometimes leave you with blocky shadows, though with careful placing (this is not an example), this can be minimised. This scene is a lot brighter than the texture lit scene, because 1. the light is white and 2. it's slightly stronger.
