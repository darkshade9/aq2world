Shane 'Fishman' Sherman


Founder of Rust
Site/Graphic Design
Content Production

Ummm...I'm a 22 year old Comp. Sci. Major at Texas A&M University. I've been editing games since....Adventure Construction Set for the Commodore C64. =) I did a lot of Doom2 editing. Released a 6 level DM pack for Doom2.(Who didnt though?). Got really into Hexen editing and made a cool DM level called HexHell. You can download it here if for some reason you have hexen still installed. =) Right when quake came out I was immediatly into level editing. My first map was created with WAX! One window, and no mouse support. It was a good editor though. =) Sometime in late 1996 I started my first quake editing page. The Quake .BSP eXpansion Project. It was definitely a success. We released the Quake .BSP eXpansion PAK #1 in early 1997. The very first custom built DM pack. I then started a new project dedicated to designing 32 player DM maps but ended up shutting it down a couple of months later. Nothing was getting done. Got the idea of Rust in a little after quake2 came out, and started working on it late December and opened it to the public on January 3rd. Currently I'm trying to find some time to make a single player episode that has been in the planning stages since quake2 came out. =) ummmm.....cant think of anything else to say....I use BSP for all my editing. Though I am currently in the process of converting to QERadiant.

Resident Artsy-Weenie and Smartass

Twenty-three years old, and I still have "Stay awhile...stay...FOREVER!" stuck in my head (for the deprived, Impossible Mission was the best game on earth that no-one knew how to play.) I've toyed about with editing games for years, but there was always something missing. I had the pleasure of having a house-design stolen by my high-school architectural design teacher, and sold to an eager homeowner (and now I have the pleasure of driving by it all the time, as the finished home is about a half mile from mine, about a half-hour outside of Sacramento, California.) Two entirely unrelated things, until Quake came along. When I saw what was possible with editing in a true 3D environment, I pretty much went nuts. The first thing I made was a replica of (what else) that house. That was, of course, when I realized that reality and good gameplay are two entirely unrelated concepts. I made a few DM maps for quake (you can find them here) but nothing too significant. Mostly exploration-type stuff. Each one was an attempt to better a specific area. (lighting, architecture, flow, etc.) Started helping Fishman out in January, and have been bugging the shit out of him ever since. I'm currently trying to dig up the time to work on an episode for Q2, but I'll leave the details on that for later. In case you haven't noticed, I tend to write too much.

Follow up: GrrandMaMa has gone on to work for RasterProductions producing the console version of Quake2 for the Nintendo64 and Sony Playstation published by Activision.

Nineteen year old self described starving level designer. I was the founder of the 3d Mapping Guild, a website for mappers of all sorts to gather. Hell i remeber posting a news update when rust opened it's doors :) Late in 1998 i closed the the doors, and moved my show to rust. I've designed levels for Quake, Quake2, and Half-life. I currently map for WardSix. Of course i'm always on the lookout for a good paying job. I play way to much Quake, and am a member of Clan 311, which has had such distinguished members as John Cash, crash, DrSleep, Kornelia, and of course little old me :) Yeah and my grammer and spelling sucks. Uhh thats all from me. Time to go eat some more ramen.

Fishman Nagger
Bitch Slapper

Twenty Four year old Art History/Anthropology major specializing in Celtic Studies. How’s that for a recipe for disaster? The moment I found out I could create my own 3D worlds to blow stuff up in I was hooked. I was raised on a steady diet of console and computer games since pong. I took up role-playing games as a serious hobby when I was twelve. I made it my mission in life to be the best MortalKombat player ever until I found PC games and Quake. As soon as I realized I had stopped watching TV, or going to school, uhh or anything else pretty much, to make and play id based games I knew I had found a new calling. I do my best to keep you all up on what’s going on in the editing world while at the same time I train myself to the meditation of editing. Ok, I admit I started on Qoole, but have since moved on to QERadiant which is the most earth shattering weapon since the nuclear bomb. Currently I do news and content for the editing sites rust, QERadiant.com and Wastelands.
