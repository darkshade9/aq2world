    Quake/Quake2 Engine Level Editors

  • Quest
    •
    Remember Quest from the old days? well its back and is one of the best editors out there. it also has the best manual for any editor available.
  • qED2
    •
    Commercial Editor made by same guy who did doomcad
  • Worldcraft
    •
    Popular editor that has quite a following. Shareware for Quake1, Commercial for quake2. Version 2.0 will be released with halflife. Made by same Ben Morris, guy who made DCK and HCK.
  • BSP(BSPHQ)
    •
    One of the best free editors out there. The one I(fishman) use. Not being updated any more though, which is a real shame. BSPHQ also has some of the best tutorials for quake1, and tons of other stuff like prefabs, files, etc.
  • Qoole
    •
    Another high quality editor. Shareware is limited to 300 brushes or something like that.
  • QuArK
    •
    Awesome shareware editor that has a large feature list. The only editor that can open BSP files(cant edit geometry though)
  • QE4Radiant
    •
    Based on id's own editor. It is being used for the development of quake3. Probably the second best editor(IMO)

    Style Guides / Editing Concepts

  • Multiplayer Level Design
    •
    The "other" half of the Designer's Guide to Multiplayer Quake Gameplay. Covers more level-specific things like terrain, topology, etc. Covers DM as well as CTF but the concepts should work with all FPS games (like the DGTMQG).
  • Designer's Guide to Multiplayer Quake Gameplay
    •
    A definite read for anyone planning on making a DM level for any first person shooter.
  • Designing Levels With Speed in Mind
    •
    Another good read for level designers. Teaches you the limitations of the quake engine and how to avoid problems. A bit dated but still a good read.
  • This Old Level
    •
    Answers the question, "where do you get your ideas for map making?" in detail from some of the boys at valve.
