 Mappack Suggestions (21:49) Updated by [ Fishman ] 

Beelzabub from Mappack has updated looking for new suggestions for Mappack. For those who don't know, Mappack is a DLL that gives map authors a large amount of new editing features for Quake 2: For example:

  • Switchable lights
  • Explosions in Deathmatch mode
  • trigger_gravity that affects the player
  • A triggerable trigger_push
  • Pushable objects
  • Player mountable func_turrets
  • Rotating func_trains
  • Quake 1 style teleporters
  • Quake 1 style wall torches
  • Quake 1 style flames
  • Quake 1's monster_dog
  • User defined model spawner
  • User defined sprites
    •


Beelzabub has done an amazing job. So head on over there and check it out and give him some new suggestions on new features.

 Forums.Gamedesign.Net Opened (8:19) Updated by [ Fishman ] 

Forums.Gamedesign.Net has officially opened. The best place to get all your editing questions answered, as well as some great discussions. bah..that was cheesy. Anyways..these forums of course replace the UBB forums and are extremely better. They may not have all the options that UBB has like the smiley faces, but that will come in due time.  Editing Q3A in Linux or MacOS? (2:19) Updated by [ raYGunn ] 

Quake3Arena will be coming in three different boxes. One for Windows, one for Linux, and one for the Mac. I fired off a mail to id to find out if they would be porting the editor for Linux and MacOS. Here's the response I got from John Carmack.

    The editor is not being ported. It is quite win32 specific. Source code will be available if anyone else wants to attempt it...
    John Carmack

Well, at least the source will be available if people using Linux or Mac's want to use id's own editor. I'm no programmer but I don't imagine porting the editors code will be an afternoon project.


 Thief: The Dark Project Editor Petition (0:04) Updated by [ Fishman ] 

Got this email a few days ago, but my computer and connection prevented me from posting it...actually..as i type this, my cable modem is offline...again...

    In case you haven't heard of it yet, Thief: The Dark Project is a fps game that strays from the norm. Rather than pulling out the BFG and blasting away baddies, the player has to act like a thief. This includes silently taking down guards, sticking to shadows, pick pocketing, and other cunning skills. Most times, entering combat will end up with you killed, and attracting more guards to the fight if you lose. For some, this might not be a fun game, but for most it is a pleasant difference. Most of the reviews have been very favorable, getting 90%'s and up. Links to these reviews can be found at:
    http://www.thief-darkproject.com/

    At the moment, no mission builder for Thief exists. However, there is a petition out on the web for gamers to sign, which will be presented to Looking Glass Studios to ask them to release the builder they used. I humbly ask for you to please report the existance of this petition on your page, in your news section. That's all. I just want word out that such a petition exists, and this will possibly attract gamers who have played Thief, and are map designers for other 3d engines. The petition can be found at:
    http://130.233.233.5/thief/

    Now, I'd like to say I'm not affiliated with any of these people, being the designers or Looking Glass Studios. Instead, I'm just a simple fan that wants to see the Thief community expand.

    Thank you for your time. :)


I've never played Thief, but i've heard from a lot of friends that it is an impressive game. So why not help the man out and sign the petition. I'm a firm believer that if you release the tools to allow end users the ability to make their own levels, etc you extend the life of the game exponentially. I just don't understand why developers don't don't understand this.
