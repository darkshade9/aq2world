 New Roger-Wilco Beta (13:28) Updated by [ The Relentless ] 

Roger-Wilco is a great internet voice communications program. It is like having a walkie talkie that communicates over the internet. It supports up to 32 simultaneous users all chatting at once. It's best feature is that it can be used WHILE you play games. I use it when I play Half-Life and Tribes with friends. It sure beats typing.

Wait. This is an editing site! What does this have to do with map-making? RW works great when you are in the playtesting part of map-making. raYGunn and I have used it when playtesting one of his maps he is working on. It really helps a lot. So enough talk. Go get it from the Roger-Wilco website.

 Disruptor Interview at PQ (7:32) Updated by [ Fishman ] 

Dont really know why I'm mentioning this since I'm sure 100% of yall saw it on blues. But oh well...Disruptor Interview at planetquake. here is the best quote related to mapping:

    The new Quake III Arena engine adds a lot of new features for mappers... "Fog for added ambiance, the entire shader architecture (makes it ridiculously easy to change textures without having to re-bsp your entire map, and some of the effects it allows us to do are just great), curves, mirrors and portals, item LOD (this makes a huge difference on larger maps with big views)..."

    Another piece of good news for mappers is that the way the back clipping plane (which causes scenery "pop-up" when you try and make a wide open space in Quake II) works has been changed for Quake III Arena.

    "zfar is calculated at level load time and buffers are resized as needed. So we can see a lot further now than was previously possible. Mind you, you still need to keep the triangle counts in line, but if you work with simple geometry, you can see *REALLY* far."


whoop!
