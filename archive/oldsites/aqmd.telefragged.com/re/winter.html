Map Name - Winter / Author - Pacman[TECH] / Map Filename - winter.zip

Reviewed by Oswald - July 19, 1999

 

 

Layout - Score 7

From Pacman[TECH] comes this adventure which takes place up in the snowy high country, with beautiful snow capped mountain peaks rising high into the air. This uniquely designed map centers around a small, partially iced over river complete with waterfall. The map is divided into four distinct sections, all which join at an icy intersection. Consider this another fast moving map, as there are many open areas, which are playable on multiple levels. You are able to climb the rocky walls throughout the entire map. Some of the overlooking areas do provide blocks of ice which act as cover when under fire. Carefully timed jumping on the icy ledges and you can maneuver most of the map. Back on the ground level, the walls rise skyward into dazzling arches and majestic cliffs. There are many pine trees on the map, some standing tall, others lying on the ground. The two features unique to this map are 'The Pit' and the partially ice covered river. If you make any attempt to climb the pit, you die. The sheet of ice covering the river is very slick, with can work both ways for you depending on your situation. As the ice breaks, it flows over another cliff into a chilling river, with chunks of ice floating in the water. There are also numerous caves to explore, which further help you navigate the map in stealth. There is also an outcropping above 'The Pit' which does provide ample cover for those sniper types, which gives a wide open view of a large portion of the playing field. Lines of fire are only interrupted by the small hills and the constant change in elevation which on the ground.

Textures & Sky - Score 7

Two main textures dominate the map, those being the brushed white snow which covers the ground and surrounding ledges on the rocky walls. The rough and rocky brown texture dominates the remaining areas, such as the walls of the map. This texture also includes touches of white, simulating snow. Those textures combined with the cold, flowing blue water are a nice contrast. The trees are modeled in a green and brown texture. The background and sky textures looks great and really give you a sense of being up in the cold, and windy mountains. The background especially, I get the sense of being in a foreign land. As for the sky, most is cover by the white and grey of the clouds as the clear clue attempts to peak through.

Prefabs - Score 5

The use of prefabs are scattered throughout the entire map. From the towering pine trees to the ice blocks on the ledges. The trees are of simple design, which help the r_speeds by keeping them low, but the trees look a bit generic. The ice blocks which you see on the ledges just seem out of place. This could be due to their square shape. They are functional, but don't do the aesthetics any favors. Any sort of shape change in those blocks to give them a bit of randomness would have made them more pleasing to the eye. Similar to the blocks which are above 'The Pit'.

Sounds - Score 8

As you spawn on the map and throughout the remainder of the round, you will have the cold and constant howling of a blowing wind. This sound fits excellent on Winter. As you approach the any of the three waterfalls you will hear the splashing of water. Both sounds fit the theme of the map and further help to strengthen the ambience. But what I want to know is, where is the ferocious scream of the Tibetan Yeti?

Lighting - Score 6

The map is set during the day, which allows the use of shadows to be cast on any freestanding object. This was well put to use, to create shadows being cast off the trees, cliffs and the ice blocks. As you approach the caverns, the interior lighting seems to give off an inviting warm glow. The caverns are darker, but have ample lighting which provide for no completely dark areas for players to hide. A very difficult map to light due to the brightness of the snow, but the author did a fine job with the use of his shadows.

Playability - Score 7

All the files unzip into their correct directories. As for the r_speeds, they are great throughout the entire map. There is one small area over near the machine gun which had r_speeds which crept up to 1000-1100, but the area which it effects is minute. The remainder of the maps has speeds which play great over the internet. Speeds ranged from 100-400 on most all of the ground while running tough the map. It does have a few areas which play a bit higher, from 500-800, generally these areas were scattered over the map, but they seemed to be contained to a small areas.

Teamplay - Score 6

It was time to get it on in the cold and winter wonderland, teams were set at 5 per side and the action began almost immediately. As the round began bullets were flying from all directions, play seemed to converge on the area near the frozen river. Those would be snipers took for the security of the cliffs to gain a better vantage point. Disposing of them turned out to be easier then on some maps, since they were left quite exposed to the elements and an opponents bullet in the backside. In the beginning, the ice cause a few problems and some easy kills, as some slid right into the own death. The play was rather fast, and with 10 players, seems there was always someone waiting around the next tree to let loose on you.

DM - Score

N/A

Originality - Score 6

The snowy setting, the blowing wind all create an original setting in which to hunt down your opponent. It is a great looking map, which offers a great time. It does not offer much in the way of surprises, rather plays straight forward. The use of the icy surfaces and the flowing waterfalls are wonderful touches, along with 'The Pit' are the main features of this map. I need to mention 'The Pit', it feels just that, bottomless and empty. It makes the map feel as if there is a void. You see the waterfall going down the side of the pit and it just ends. If there is one thing I would like to see it would be more caves, to offer more access to the map. Great job on the map aside from that.

General Feel - Score 6

This map is a wonderful change from the daily grind of the city block maps. It is not an overly large map, which make the action fast and furious and the frags pile up. I sure hope we see this map pop up on some servers. It should keep players coming back for more action, round after round. Remember watch that ice, it can take one by surprise at the most inopportune time.

Final Rating - Score 6.4 (58/9)
